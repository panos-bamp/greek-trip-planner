# Best Hotels in Skopelos, Greece: Our Top Picks for 2026

Skopelos is the island that Mamma Mia put on the map — and that deserves to be there for reasons that have nothing to do with Meryl Streep. The film was shot here in 2008, and the clifftop chapel of Agios Ioannis Kastri, where the wedding scene took place, has since become an Instagram pilgrimage site. But the island beneath the movie set is something richer, quieter, and more enduring: the greenest island in the Aegean, with pine forests so dense they create their own shade, plum orchards that produce the famous Skopelos prune, and a harbor town so perfectly composed that the movie production designers barely needed to change a thing.

Skopelos Town tumbles down a hillside to the water in a cascade of white houses with slate roofs, brightly painted shutters, and balconies draped in bougainvillea. Over 120 churches — some barely larger than a garden shed, others centuries-old monasteries — dot the town and the surrounding hills. The harbor is small and genuine: fishing boats unload the morning catch, the ferry arrives with a blast of its horn, and the waterfront tavernas serve the kind of food that comes from a kitchen where someone's grandmother is still in charge.

The beaches are different from the Cycladic model. Pine trees grow to the waterline, creating natural canopies over pebble-and-sand coves where the water is clean enough to see the seabed at five meters. Panormos, Milia, and Kastani (the Mamma Mia beach) are the most popular — pine-backed, turquoise-watered, and equipped with just enough tavernas to keep you fed without breaking the forest spell.

## Boutique & Resort Hotels

### Adrina Resort & Spa

The most complete hotel on Skopelos — a beachfront resort on the coast between Skopelos Town and Panormos with a spa, pool, private beach, and rooms that have been designed with a contemporary Aegean aesthetic. Adrina is the rare Skopelos property that combines genuine resort facilities with the island's forest-and-sea character. The beach is pebbly and pine-backed, the spa is well-equipped, and the restaurant serves creative Greek cuisine with local ingredients.

The location puts you between the town (about 10 minutes by car) and the popular south-coast beaches (Panormos, Milia, Kastani). The setting — pine forest meeting turquoise bay — captures Skopelos at its most beautiful.

**Price range:** €180–400/night
**Best for:** Couples, honeymooners, resort seekers, anyone wanting the island's most polished experience
**Good to know:** The beachfront location means a car is useful for reaching Skopelos Town and the other beaches. The spa and pool provide all-day options. The property has two sections — the Beach and the Resort; the Resort is the more intimate and recently updated.

[Check prices for Adrina Resort & Spa on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/adrina-resort-spa.html)

### Skopelos Village

A hillside hotel above Skopelos Town with a pool, well-designed rooms in a bungalow layout, and views across the Sporades that make the pool terrace one of the best sunset spots on the island. The architecture uses local stone and wood, and the layout — individual units set among gardens — creates a village-like atmosphere that gives the property its name.

**Price range:** €120–280/night
**Best for:** Couples wanting a pool with views, design-conscious travelers, anyone wanting a hilltop retreat near town
**Good to know:** The hilltop location means a drive or steep walk to Skopelos Town (about 10 minutes downhill). The pool view is the hotel's best feature. Breakfast uses local products. A car is useful.

## Skopelos Town Hotels

### Pension Sotos

A family-run pension in the lanes of Skopelos Town — the kind of property where the owner meets you at the harbor, carries your bag up the lane, shows you to a room with a small balcony overlooking the rooftops, and tells you which taverna to eat at tonight (his cousin's, naturally, which turns out to be excellent). The rooms are simple, clean, and full of the kind of character that comes from a building that has been lived in and loved for generations.

Staying at Sotos is staying inside Skopelos Town's fabric — the morning light through the shutters, the church bells marking the hours, the scent of jasmine from the courtyard below. The town's harbor, restaurants, and churches are a short walk through the lanes.

**Price range:** €50–110/night
**Best for:** Budget travelers, couples wanting authentic town atmosphere, solo visitors, anyone who values warmth over amenities
**Good to know:** Simple rooms — manage expectations. The lanes are steep and narrow; pack light. No pool, no restaurant. The town's tavernas and cafés are your amenities. The family's warmth is consistently the highlight of guest reviews.

### Hotel Denise

A clean, well-maintained hotel in Skopelos Town near the harbor — practical, affordable, and positioned for travelers who want the town's restaurants and waterfront within easy walking distance. Rooms are simple and recently updated. The breakfast is included and adequate.

**Price range:** €60–140/night
**Best for:** Practical travelers, families, budget-conscious visitors wanting a central town base
**Good to know:** No pool. The harbor is a short walk. The bus to the south-coast beaches departs from nearby. Rooms are functional — the town is the attraction.

**Good to know:** The rooftop terrace is the highlight — evening drinks here with the harbor below. Rooms are well-maintained. The waterfront restaurants are a two-minute walk.

## Beach Hotels

### Milia Bay Hotel

Directly on Milia Beach — one of the most beautiful beaches on Skopelos, where pine trees grow to the water's edge and the pebble-and-sand shore curves around a turquoise bay. The hotel is simple and practical: clean rooms, a beachfront restaurant, and the decisive advantage of waking up with the pine forest behind you and the Aegean in front.

Milia is adjacent to Kastani Beach — the Mamma Mia filming beach — which is a short walk or drive through the forest. The two beaches together create the island's most concentrated beach area.

**Price range:** €80–180/night
**Best for:** Beach lovers, nature seekers, Mamma Mia pilgrims wanting proximity to the film locations
**Good to know:** The beach setting is the main amenity — basic rooms, honest prices. Skopelos Town is about 15 minutes by car. The pine-forest shade is a genuine advantage. The beach restaurant is decent for lunch.

## Practical Tips for Skopelos Hotels

**Getting there.** No airport — Skopelos is reached by ferry. From [Skiathos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/skiathos-travel-guide) (which has an airport): about 45 minutes by hydrofoil. From [Volos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/volos-travel-guide): about 2–3 hours. From Alonissos: about 30 minutes. The ferry arrives at either Skopelos Town (main port) or Loutraki/Glossa (northwest tip).

**A car or scooter is useful.** The island is about 17 km long. The south-coast beaches (Panormos, Milia, Kastani) are about 15 minutes from Skopelos Town by car. Agios Ioannis Kastri (the Mamma Mia church) is about 20 minutes. A bus service operates in summer, but a rental vehicle adds significant flexibility.

**The Mamma Mia church.** Agios Ioannis Kastri sits on a dramatic rock on the northeast coast, reached by 105 steps carved into the cliff face. The view from the top — and the chapel itself — is worth the climb regardless of the movie connection. Go early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds and heat.

**When to visit.** June and September are ideal. July–August are the busiest months, particularly around the Mamma Mia filming sites. May and October are quiet and beautiful — the forests are most alive in spring. Skopelos has genuine shoulder-season appeal. See our [Greece weather guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/greece-weather-by-month).

**The food.** Skopelos plums are famous — the dried prune version appears in local dishes and as a sweet preserve. The tavernas serve fresh fish, goat from the mountain, and the kind of home-cooked Greek food that benefits from the island's agricultural abundance. The Skopelos cheese pie (skopelitiki tyropita) is a local specialty.

**Combining with other islands.** Skopelos pairs naturally with [Skiathos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/skiathos-travel-guide) (45 min by ferry, has the airport) and Alonissos (30 min, the marine park island). A Sporades route from [Volos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/volos-travel-guide) — adding [Pelion](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/pelion-travel-guide) on the mainland — is one of the most varied and underrated Greek itineraries.
