# Best Hotels in Corfu, Greece: Our Top Picks for 2026

Corfu is the Greek island that breaks the stereotype. No whitewashed cubes, no blue domes, no volcanic cliffs. Instead: Venetian fortresses, French arcades, British cricket pitches, and four million olive trees casting silver-green light across a landscape so lush it would look at home in Tuscany. The architecture is Italianate. The cuisine has Venetian roots. The town has a UNESCO World Heritage stamp. And the hotel scene ranges from some of the most sophisticated all-inclusive resorts in Europe to tiny guesthouses in fishing villages where Lawrence Durrell wrote his novels.

This diversity is Corfu's greatest strength and its challenge. Choosing between a Venetian townhouse in the old town, a five-star resort on the coast, and a pebble-cove boutique on the northeast coast is not a matter of quality — it's a matter of what kind of holiday you want. All three are excellent. They're just fundamentally different experiences.

## Luxury & Resort Hotels

### Ikos Dassia

There is a before-and-after in Greek hospitality, and Ikos Dassia is the dividing line. Before Ikos, all-inclusive in Greece meant buffet restaurants, watered-down drinks, and the vague sense that you were paying for convenience at the expense of quality. Ikos redefined the model entirely. The dining here includes restaurants created by Michelin-starred chefs. The drinks are genuine — Greek wines, premium spirits, cocktails made by bartenders who care. The beach is private and immaculate. The spa is world-class. The kids' club is comprehensive enough that parents feel genuinely free.

Set on the east coast between Corfu Town and the northeast, Ikos Dassia sits in lush grounds that roll down to its own beach. Rooms and suites are spacious and well-designed, with the kind of contemporary Mediterranean aesthetic that photographs beautifully and lives comfortably. The all-inclusive package covers everything — meals, drinks, room service, a local wine-tasting experience, even a MINI Cooper to explore the island for a day.

The price is high. But the all-inclusive model means you genuinely don't pay for anything once you arrive, and the quality justifies the investment. For families or couples who want a self-contained, luxurious beach holiday without the logistical effort of planning daily meals and activities, Ikos is the best in class in Greece.

**Price range:** €350–900/night (all-inclusive)
**Best for:** Families, couples wanting luxury all-inclusive, travelers who value not thinking about logistics
**Good to know:** Everything is included — meals, drinks, room service, bikes, water sports. The MINI Cooper day-use is a brilliant touch. Book well ahead for July–August. See our [all-inclusive Greece guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/all-inclusive-trip-to-greece) for more options.

[Check prices for Ikos Dassia on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/ikos-dassia.html)

### Grecotel Corfu Imperial

The grand resort on the Kommeno peninsula — Corfu's most exclusive address. Grecotel Corfu Imperial sits on a wooded headland surrounded by sea on three sides, with private beaches, a spa, multiple restaurants, and the kind of manicured grounds that blur the line between hotel and estate. The architecture is low-rise and integrated into the landscape, giving each bungalow and suite a sense of seclusion.

The Kommeno location is special — a peninsula that juts into the Ionian Sea with views in every direction, yet only ten minutes from Corfu Town. The main pool overlooks the sea, the restaurants serve Greek and international cuisine at a standard that exceeds resort expectations, and the overall atmosphere is one of restrained, established luxury.

**Price range:** €250–700/night
**Best for:** Luxury seekers, couples, families wanting a prestige resort in a natural setting
**Good to know:** The peninsula is beautiful but isolated — you'll want a car or use the hotel's transfer service for Corfu Town and other parts of the island. The private beaches are the best feature — uncrowded and well-serviced.

[Check prices for Grecotel Corfu Imperial on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/grecotel-corfu-imperial.html)

### Kontokali Bay Resort & Spa

A five-star resort on the east coast in Kontokali, set on its own peninsula with a private beach, beautiful gardens, and a spa. The position — sheltered in Gouvia Bay with views across to the mainland mountains — is peaceful and scenic. Rooms have been recently renovated, the waterfront restaurant is atmospheric, and the family facilities are genuine rather than tokenistic.

Kontokali Bay occupies the middle ground between the ultra-luxury of Ikos and the boutique intimacy of the old-town hotels. It's a proper resort — pools, beach, spa, dining — but scaled to feel personal rather than industrial. For families and couples wanting resort comfort without losing the sense that they're on a Greek island rather than anywhere in the world, this is a strong choice.

**Price range:** €130–300/night
**Best for:** Families, couples wanting a resort base with character, mid-range luxury seekers
**Good to know:** The peninsula setting means a car is useful for exploring the island. The hotel's beach is a highlight — calm, clean, and backed by gardens. Corfu Town is a ten-minute drive.

[Check prices for Kontokali Bay on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/kontokali-bay-resort.html)

## Boutique & Character Hotels

### Siorra Vittoria Boutique Hotel

The finest boutique hotel inside the old town walls — and one of the most atmospheric stays on any Greek island. Siorra Vittoria occupies a restored Venetian townhouse in the heart of Corfu Town, with thirteen individually designed rooms, original frescoes on some ceilings, period furniture mixed with modern comforts, and a courtyard where breakfast is served under lemon trees. The building is stunning — the kind of property where you find yourself photographing the staircase, the doorframes, the light falling through a window.

The location is perfect: steps from the Liston, the museums, and the old town's labyrinthine lanes. The staff know the town intimately and offer the kind of personal recommendations — a particular taverna, a lane you haven't found yet, a church worth seeing at a specific hour — that transform a visit into an experience.

**Price range:** €150–320/night
**Best for:** Couples, history lovers, architecture enthusiasts, anyone who values character over standardized comfort
**Good to know:** No elevator, and the historic staircase is narrow. Rooms vary significantly in size — ask for one of the larger suites. Street noise can filter in; courtyard-facing rooms are quieter.

[Check prices for Siorra Vittoria on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/siorra-vittoria.html)

### White House Kalami

There is something poetic about staying in the house where Lawrence Durrell wrote *Prospero's Cell*. The White House sits directly on the waterfront in Kalami — a small bay on the northeast coast — with the sea lapping at the terrace and Albania visible across the narrow strait. The rooms upstairs are simple but atmospheric: whitewashed walls, wooden shutters, views across to the mountains. The taverna below serves excellent Greek food on a terrace that may be the most romantic dining spot on the island.

This is not a luxury hotel. There's no pool, no spa, no room service. What there is: a place with a story, a view that has seduced writers for a century, and an authenticity that no resort can manufacture. The bay at Kalami, with its clear water and cypress-backed shoreline, hasn't changed much since the Durrells' time. Neither has the feeling of swimming in it.

**Price range:** €100–200/night
**Best for:** Literature lovers, couples seeking atmosphere over amenities, travelers who value story and setting
**Good to know:** Very few rooms — book well in advance. No car access to the northeast coast villages means a car is essential. The surrounding bays — Agni, Nissaki, Kerasia — offer some of the best swimming on the island.

[Check prices for White House Kalami on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/the-white-house.html)

### Cavalieri Hotel

A four-star hotel in a landmark building on the Spianada — Corfu Town's grand central square and the largest in Greece. The rooms are classically decorated, well-maintained, and comfortable. The real draw is the rooftop bar, which has one of the best views in town: across the Spianada to the Old Fortress, the harbor, and the mountains of Albania beyond. Sunset drinks here are a non-negotiable even if you don't stay.

Cavalieri occupies the intersection of history and practicality. The building has heritage, the location is central to everything in the old town, and the prices — for a four-star on a historic square with that rooftop view — represent genuine value.

**Price range:** €120–250/night
**Best for:** First-time visitors, couples wanting a central old-town base, anyone who values a great rooftop view
**Good to know:** Historic building means some compact rooms. The Spianada can be lively during festivals and summer evenings — atmosphere for most, noise for some.

[Check prices for Cavalieri Hotel on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/cavalieri.html)

### Bella Venezia

A neoclassical mansion turned elegant hotel, set just off the Spianada in a quiet corner of the old town. Rooms are refined without being fussy — wooden floors, tasteful fabrics, some with wrought-iron balconies overlooking the garden. Breakfast is served in a leafy courtyard that feels like a private garden. The staff are warm, knowledgeable, and proud of the hotel in a way that shows.

Bella Venezia represents the kind of European mid-range hotel that's becoming increasingly rare — genuinely elegant, personally run, and priced fairly. It's the hotel you recommend to friends without hesitation.

**Price range:** €100–220/night
**Best for:** Couples, families, budget-conscious travelers wanting old-town elegance
**Good to know:** One of the best value-for-quality hotels in Corfu Town. The garden courtyard is genuinely lovely. Parking is available nearby but limited — arrange through the hotel.

[Check prices for Bella Venezia on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/bella-venezia.html)

## Family & Beach Resort Hotels

### Mayor Capo Di Corfu

A large resort hotel west of Sidari, set on a headland with its own beach, multiple pools (including kids' pools and slides), and the kind of manicured grounds that turn a family holiday from an exercise in logistics into an actual vacation. Rooms are modern and spacious, the dining options cover picky eaters of all ages, and the animation team keeps children occupied without driving parents to distraction.

The headland location is beautiful — surrounded by sea, private and well-maintained. The trade-off is isolation from Corfu's villages and the old town (you'll need a car for everything outside the resort). But for families wanting a self-contained beach holiday where the hardest decision is which pool to use, Mayor Capo delivers.

**Price range:** €120–280/night
**Best for:** Families with children, beach-resort holidaymakers, anyone wanting convenience and facilities
**Good to know:** The resort's own beach is small but private and clean. A car is needed for exploring the island. The north coast's family-friendly beaches at [Sidari and Acharavi](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-corfu) are within easy reach. ### Akrotiri Beach Hotel (Paleokastritsa)

The best-positioned hotel in Paleokastritsa — Corfu's most famous coastal area, where turquoise coves meet forested cliffs. The hotel sits directly above one of the main bays, with terraced gardens dropping down to the water. Rooms are comfortable with balconies looking out over the turquoise sea. The restaurant terrace is a highlight — dinner with that view as the sun sets over the open Ionian is unforgettable.

Paleokastritsa is where Corfu's coast becomes genuinely dramatic — the water colors here look digitally enhanced but aren't. Glass-bottom boat trips to sea caves, swimming in sheltered coves, and the Monastery of Theotokos on the promontory above all make this corner of the island special.

**Price range:** €100–220/night
**Best for:** Beach lovers, nature enthusiasts, families, photographers, anyone wanting dramatic coastal scenery
**Good to know:** The terraced hillside means lots of steps — not ideal for mobility issues. Book a sea-view room; the difference between sea-view and garden-view here is everything.

[Check prices for Akrotiri Beach on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/akrotiri-beach-paleokastritsa.html)

## Budget Hotels Worth Booking

### Hotel Bretagne

A clean, simple, family-run hotel in the new town of Corfu, a ten-minute walk from the old town gates. Rooms are basic but honest — air-conditioned, comfortable beds, functional bathrooms. The family who runs it is friendly and helpful with practical advice. The price is the point: enough savings to spend on a boat trip to [Paxos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/paxos-travel-guide), a car rental for the west coast, or three extra dinners at the old-town tavernas that make Corfu's food scene so rewarding.

**Price range:** €50–110/night
**Best for:** Budget travelers, practical visitors who'd rather spend on experiences, longer stays
**Good to know:** Outside the old town walls, so the atmosphere is less atmospheric than a Venetian-lane hotel. The practical trade-off: easier parking, lower prices, and a quieter night's sleep.

## Practical Tips for Corfu Hotels

**Old town vs coast vs northeast.** Three fundamentally different experiences. The old town is cultural, walkable, and restaurant-rich. The coast (Paleokastritsa, Glyfada, Agios Gordios) is beach-forward and scenic. The northeast (Kassiopi, Kalami, Agni) is quiet, romantic, and literary. Choose based on your priorities, or split your stay.

**Airport convenience.** Corfu's airport is only 2 km from Corfu Town — one of the shortest airport-to-town transfers in Greece. You can be in your old-town hotel within twenty minutes of landing. This makes Corfu uniquely practical for short trips and late arrivals.

**Day trips.** [Paxos and Antipaxos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/paxos-travel-guide) — the tiny islands south of Corfu with Caribbean-grade turquoise water — are an essential day trip. Boats take about an hour. [Book a Paxos & Blue Caves cruise from Corfu](https://www.getyourguide.com/corfu-l405/from-corfu-day-cruise-to-paxos-antipaxos-the-blue-caves-t166244/).

**When to visit.** May–June and September–October are ideal. Corfu is further north than the Cyclades, with milder summers. Spring is spectacular — wildflowers, green everywhere. Easter on Corfu is legendary, with dramatic celebrations unique to the island. Check our [Greece weather guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/greece-weather-by-month).

**Combining with other islands.** Corfu pairs with [Kefalonia](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/kefalonia-travel-guide) and [Zakynthos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/zakynthos-travel-guide) for an Ionian route, or ferry to the mainland at Igoumenitsa and drive south through the [Peloponnese](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/greece-road-trip). See [Corfu vs Crete](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/corfu-vs-crete) for comparison. Let our [AI trip planner](https://greektriplanner.me/ai-trip-planner) build your route.
