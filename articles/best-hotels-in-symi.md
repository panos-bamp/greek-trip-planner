No resort infrastructure. No nightlife beyond a glass of wine at a harbor taverna. What Symi offers instead is architectural beauty, crystalline swimming coves, and an atmosphere of quiet elegance that resets your pace entirely.

## ARTICLE BODY

# Best Hotels in Symi, Greece: Our Top Picks for 2026

The first time you see Symi's harbor — the moment the ferry rounds the headland and the bay opens like a theatre curtain — you understand why painters came here. The harbor of Gialos is a horseshoe of neoclassical mansions rising in tiers from the waterfront, each building painted in a different shade of the warm Mediterranean palette: deep ochre, faded terracotta, dusty pink, sunbleached cream, cornflower blue. The effect is of a place where color itself is a building material, and where the combination of painted stone, blue water, and Aegean light creates a composition so perfect it seems designed rather than evolved.

It was designed, in a sense. Symi's prosperity came from sponge diving and shipbuilding in the 18th and 19th centuries, and the wealthy merchants who built these mansions intended them to announce their success — to anyone arriving by sea — through architectural beauty. The tradition stuck. Today, the mansions that survive (the island's population has shrunk dramatically since its peak) have been restored as homes, hotels, and restaurants, and the harbor's visual integrity is protected by a building code that requires new construction to match the neoclassical style.

The day-trip crowds from [Rhodes](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/rhodes-travel-guide) see Symi for a few hours — enough to photograph the harbor, eat lunch, and buy a sponge. But the island reveals itself slowly, and the rewards of staying overnight are substantial: the harbor at sunset when the mansions glow gold, the Kali Strata staircase (500 stone steps to Ano Symi) at dawn when the light is theatrical, and the swimming coves — Nanou, Marathounda, St. George — reached by water taxi and empty of everyone except you and the Aegean.

## Harbor Boutique Hotels

### The Old Markets

The most design-forward hotel on Symi — a restored building on the harbor waterfront where contemporary interior design meets neoclassical architecture. The conversion is intelligent: original stonework and architectural details are preserved while the rooms have been fitted with modern furniture, quality textiles, and the kind of considered lighting that signals genuine design intent. The harbor views from the upper rooms and the terrace are spectacular.

The Old Markets represents what Symi's hotel scene is becoming: properties that honor the island's architectural heritage while offering the comfort and aesthetics that contemporary travelers expect. The location — directly on the harbor — means you're at the center of Gialos's restaurant and café scene.

**Price range:** €160–350/night
**Best for:** Design lovers, couples, anyone wanting Symi's most refined stay
**Good to know:** Harbor-front means some evening noise from the restaurants below. The upper-floor rooms with harbor views are essential — the view defines the stay. The harbor restaurants are steps away.

### Hotel Aliki

A neoclassical mansion on the Gialos waterfront — one of the grand sponge-merchant residences that define Symi's architectural character — restored into a boutique hotel with rooms that feature original ceiling paintings, wooden floors, and balconies overlooking the harbor. Hotel Aliki is the classic Symi stay: the kind of property where the building's history is visible in every surface and the harbor view from the balcony is the reason you came.

**Price range:** €130–280/night
**Best for:** Couples, architecture lovers, anyone wanting to sleep in a genuine neoclassical Symi mansion
**Good to know:** The neoclassical character means rooms vary in size and layout — some are grand, others more compact. The harbor-facing balcony rooms are essential. The building's original ceiling frescoes are remarkable.

### Opera House Hotel

A small, intimate hotel in a restored building near the harbor with rooms that blend period character with contemporary comfort. The name references the building's theatrical history, and the atmosphere retains a sense of performance — as though the harbor visible through the window were a stage set. Rooms are individually designed, and the scale (around 10 rooms) ensures personal attention.

**Price range:** €120–260/night
**Best for:** Romantic couples, culture lovers, anyone wanting intimate harbor-front atmosphere
**Good to know:** Small property — book ahead for summer. No pool (pools don't exist on Symi). The harbor tavernas and the Kali Strata staircase are steps away.

A well-maintained waterfront hotel on the Gialos harbor with clean, comfortable rooms, some with balconies overlooking the water, and the kind of reliable mid-range quality that makes a harbor-front base both pleasant and affordable. ## Ano Symi (Upper Town) Hotels

### Iapetos Village

A collection of traditional restored houses in Ano Symi — the upper town reached by the Kali Strata staircase — converted into self-catering accommodation with terraces that look down over the rooftops of Gialos to the harbor and the sea beyond. The views from Ano Symi are among the most spectacular on the island — the entire harbor laid out below, the Aegean stretching to the Turkish coast, and the light shifting across the mansions as the day progresses.

Staying in Ano Symi means committing to the Kali Strata — 500 stone steps between you and the harbor, which you'll descend for dinner and ascend for bed. The climb becomes a ritual, and the view from the top, earned by effort, feels more significant than any elevator-delivered panorama.

**Price range:** €80–180/night
**Best for:** Couples, walkers, photographers, anyone willing to climb for the best views on the island
**Good to know:** The 500-step Kali Strata is real — the climb is beautiful but physical. Not suitable for mobility issues or heavy luggage (though local porterage can be arranged). The upper town is quieter and has fewer restaurants — dinner requires the descent to Gialos.

## Practical Tips for Symi Hotels

**Getting there.** No airport. Catamaran from [Rhodes](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-rhodes): about 1 hour. Conventional ferry: about 2 hours. Daily service in summer; reduced in winter. Some ferries connect Symi to [Kos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/kos-travel-guide) and other Dodecanese islands. Symi is also accessible from Turkey (Datça peninsula, seasonal).

**Stay overnight.** This is the single most important advice. Day-trippers from Rhodes flood the harbor from about 10 AM to 4 PM. By late afternoon, the town transforms — the harbor becomes intimate, the light turns golden, the restaurants fill with locals and overnight guests, and Symi reveals the atmosphere that the daytime crowds obscure. At minimum, stay one night; two or three is ideal.

**Swimming.** Symi has no sandy beaches. Swimming is from pebble coves, rock platforms, and small bays around the coast — reached by water taxi from Gialos harbor. Nanou Bay, Marathounda, and St. George's Bay are the most popular. The water is crystalline. Water taxis run regularly in summer and are part of the Symi experience.

**The Monastery of Panormitis.** The large monastery on the south coast — dedicated to the Archangel Michael — is the island's most important religious site and a major pilgrimage destination. The day-trip boats from Rhodes usually stop here. It's also reachable by road from Gialos (about 30 minutes by car or bus).

**When to visit.** May–June and September–October for the best balance of weather and crowd levels. July–August are hot and the day-trip crowds are at their peak. Symi has a genuine off-season character — some hotels and restaurants stay open through winter, and the empty harbor in low season has an atmospheric beauty. **Combining with other islands.** Symi pairs naturally with [Rhodes](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-rhodes) (1 hour by catamaran — the practical combination). A Dodecanese route of Rhodes–Symi–[Kos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/kos-travel-guide)–[Patmos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/patmos-travel-guide) covers the region's range from medieval city to neoclassical harbor to ancient history to spiritual island.
