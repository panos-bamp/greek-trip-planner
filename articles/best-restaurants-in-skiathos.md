## ARTICLE BODY

# Best Restaurants in Skiathos, Greece: Where to Eat in 2026

Skiathos eats better than its 60-beach, party-island reputation suggests — and the food scene is improving faster than the guidebooks acknowledge. The island that once served mainly as refueling infrastructure between beach sessions and bar sessions has developed a restaurant culture in Skiathos Town that rewards genuine exploration: creative Greek kitchens using Sporades ingredients with care, meze spots where the small-plate tradition is practiced with skill, and fish tavernas where the morning catch arrives from waters so clean that the Sporades were designated a marine park.

The pine forests that make Skiathos's beaches special also shape the dining experience. The beach tavernas — Troulos, Agia Paraskevi, Koukounaries — sit beneath pine canopies, and the combination of eating grilled fish under resinous shade with the turquoise Aegean stretching below creates a lunch experience that the Cycladic dry-rock beaches, for all their beauty, can't replicate.

## Skiathos Town — Creative & Contemporary

### Marmita

The most ambitious restaurant on Skiathos — a creative Greek kitchen that applies seasonal thinking and genuine culinary care to Sporades ingredients. The fish is local. The vegetables are sourced with attention. The wine list features Greek producers with depth. And the cooking — modern Greek with Mediterranean touches — positions Marmita as the island's answer to the creative dining that the Cyclades take for granted.

The space is contemporary and pleasant, on a lane behind the main Papadiamantis strip — a physical distance from the tourist flow that mirrors the culinary distance from the tourist-standard restaurants.

**Cuisine:** Creative Greek, seasonal
**Price range:** €28–45/person
**Best for:** Couples, food-curious travelers, the island's most serious dinner
**Good to know:** Reserve for dinner. The backstreet location is a short walk from the Papadiamantis strip. The menu changes with the season and the catch. The wine list rewards curiosity.

### 1901

A meze-and-wine bar in the backstreets of Skiathos Town that captures the island's evolving evening culture — creative small plates, a wine list that's well above the island average, and an atmosphere that's convivial without being the loud, party-oriented energy of the main strip. The dishes are designed for sharing, the portions are generous by meze standards, and the wine guidance from the staff is genuine.

**Cuisine:** Creative meze, wine bar
**Price range:** €20–35/person
**Best for:** Wine lovers, couples, groups wanting shared-plate dining
**Good to know:** The backstreet location is the appeal — away from the Papadiamantis foot traffic. The wine list features Greek producers you won't find on the tourist-facing menus. Reserve for summer weekends.

## Skiathos Town — Traditional & Seafood

### Taverna Agnanteio

A hilltop taverna above the old harbor — perched on the hill behind the Bourtzi peninsula with views across the harbor, the town, and the Sporades islands beyond. The food is traditional Greek: grilled meats, fresh fish, meze, salads. The cooking is reliable rather than revolutionary, but the setting — the panoramic terrace, the evening light on the harbor, the islands fading into the Aegean twilight — elevates everything into an experience.

Agnanteio is the restaurant that Skiathos locals consider the town's most reliable dinner — quality food, honest prices, the best view.

**Cuisine:** Traditional Greek, hilltop panorama
**Price range:** €18–30/person
**Best for:** View-dinner seekers, the locals' favorite, families
**Good to know:** The hilltop location requires a walk uphill — about 10 minutes from the harbor. The terrace view is the reward. Reserve for sunset tables. The lamb chops and the grilled fish are the strongest orders.

### Bourtzi Restaurant

Set on the Bourtzi peninsula — the small fortress that divides the harbor into two bays — with a quieter, more refined atmosphere than the main waterfront strip. The fish is fresh, the Greek-Mediterranean cooking is a step above the harbor average, and the peninsula position provides a sense of separation from the town's bustle while remaining central.

**Cuisine:** Greek-Mediterranean, seafood
**Price range:** €22–38/person
**Best for:** Couples wanting harbor seafood in a quieter setting, the Bourtzi experience
**Good to know:** The Bourtzi peninsula is a short walk from the main harbor. The setting is quieter than the main waterfront — less foot traffic, more composed. The fish is priced by weight. Reserve for harbor-view tables.

### Ouzeri Jimmy's (Old Harbor)

A backstreet ouzeri near the old harbor that serves honest meze — grilled octopus, fried cheese, small fish, seasonal dips — at prices that make the harbor-front restaurants seem inflated. Jimmy's is the kind of place where the tables are small, the portions are generous, and the tsipouro flows freely as the evening warms up. The clientele is mixed — locals, returning visitors, and the travelers who discovered it through word of mouth.

**Cuisine:** Traditional meze, ouzeri
**Price range:** €12–22/person
**Best for:** Budget meze lovers, the old-harbor atmosphere, authentic Sporades social eating
**Good to know:** No reservations. The old-harbor backstreet location is a 5-minute walk from the main strip. Cash preferred. The meze format (4–6 plates for two) is the way to eat. The tsipouro is the accompaniment.

### Taverna Mesogios

A seafood taverna slightly set back from the harbor — less prominently positioned than the front-row restaurants, with prices that reflect the difference and fish quality that doesn't. The cooking is traditional Greek seafood (grilled, fried, simply sauced), and the value proposition — honest fish at fair prices — makes it the smarter choice for travelers who want the harbor-area experience without paying the front-row premium.

**Cuisine:** Traditional seafood
**Price range:** €16–28/person
**Best for:** Value-seeking fish lovers, practical travelers, the harbor area at fairer prices
**Good to know:** Slightly set back from the waterfront — a practical advantage in price. The fish quality is equal to the more prominent restaurants. The fried calamari is consistently good.

## Beach Tavernas

### Mandraki Taverna (Koukounaries area)

A pine-shaded beach taverna near Koukounaries — the island's most famous beach — where the tables sit beneath a canopy of pine trees and the water visible through the branches is the specific shade of turquoise that makes the Sporades photographs sell travel magazines. The food is beach-taverna standard: grilled fish, salads, fried calamari, cold beer. The setting does the elevating.

Mandraki represents the quintessential Skiathos food experience: pine shade, turquoise water, fresh fish, and the unhurried pace of a Sporades beach afternoon.

**Cuisine:** Greek beach taverna, pine-shaded
**Price range:** €14–26/person
**Best for:** Beach-day lunches, the quintessential Skiathos food setting, families
**Good to know:** Accessible by the south-coast bus (regular service from Skiathos Town to Koukounaries). Koukounaries Beach is a short walk. The pine shade is a genuine advantage in summer heat. The fish is the best order.

### Taverna Troulos Beach

A beachfront taverna at Troulos — one of the most family-friendly south-coast beaches — with tables on the sand and a menu of Greek beach-taverna standards. The setting is pleasant, the water is calm and shallow (ideal for children), and the food is honest without being ambitious. The value is in the combination: beach, swim, eat, repeat.

**Cuisine:** Greek beach taverna
**Price range:** €12–24/person
**Best for:** Families, beach-day lunches, the south-coast bus-connected dining circuit
**Good to know:** Troulos is about 10 minutes from Skiathos Town by bus. The beach is sandy and sheltered. The taverna is simple and reliable. The bus service makes car-free beach-hopping practical.

## Budget

### Skiathos Souvlaki & Gyros

Multiple souvlaki shops operate on and around the Papadiamantis strip — the gyros wraps and souvlaki skewers provide the budget backbone for travelers spending their dining budget selectively (one creative dinner, one beach lunch, and souvlaki for the rest). At €4–7 per wrap, the quality is good and the late-night hours serve the post-bar crowd.

**Cuisine:** Souvlaki, gyros
**Price range:** €4–7/wrap
**Best for:** Budget travelers, late-night fuel, the Papadiamantis-strip refueling stop

## Practical Tips for Eating in Skiathos

**Skiathos Town vs the beaches.** Skiathos Town for the best restaurants — creative, traditional, meze, seafood. The south-coast beach tavernas for lunch — pine-shaded, beachfront, connected by the excellent bus service. Eat dinner in town; eat lunch on the beach. The bus makes the combination effortless.

**The bus-connected food trail.** The south-coast bus runs every 15–20 minutes from Skiathos Town to Koukounaries, stopping at all the major beaches. You can eat at a different beach taverna every day without renting a car — Agia Paraskevi, Troulos, Koukounaries, each with its own taverna.

**When to eat.** Beach tavernas: lunch (1–3 PM). Skiathos Town restaurants: dinner (8:30 PM onward). The harbor-front fills early — the sunset-facing tables are claimed by 7:30 PM. The backstreet restaurants fill by 9:30 PM.

**The Lalaria boat trip.** The excursion boat from Skiathos Town to Lalaria Beach (the dramatic north-coast pebble beach accessible only by boat) and around the island is one of the best Skiathos experiences. Pack snacks — there are no tavernas at Lalaria. Eat a proper lunch back in town after the trip. See our [Skiathos travel guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/skiathos-travel-guide).

**Combine with Skopelos.** [Skopelos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/skopelos-travel-guide) (45 min by ferry) has a food scene with a different character — greener, quieter, more Mamma-Mia. A Skiathos–Skopelos combination covers the Sporades range from lively to contemplative.
