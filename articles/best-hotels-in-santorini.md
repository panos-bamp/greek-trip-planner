## ARTICLE BODY

# Best Hotels in Santorini, Greece: Our Top Picks for 2026

There's a reason Santorini appears on every bucket list, and it's not marketing. It's geology. The island is the eastern rim of a volcanic caldera — the remnant of one of the most powerful eruptions in recorded history — and the villages that cling to the cliff edge look out over a flooded crater of such scale and beauty that no photograph fully captures it. The water is impossibly blue. The cliffs drop three hundred meters. The volcanic islands at the center seem to float in the haze. And the sunset, particularly from Oia, turns the entire scene into something that feels choreographed by a higher power.

The hotel industry understood this early. Santorini's caldera-side hotels are among the most photographed and desired in the world — cave suites carved into volcanic rock, infinity pools that merge with the Aegean, terraces where breakfast arrives with a view worth more than the meal. The competition among these properties has driven quality to extraordinary levels. The best Santorini hotels are not just good Greek hotels. They're among the best small hotels anywhere.

But Santorini is also an island of contrasts. While the caldera villages command €300–1,000+ per night, the east coast offers clean, cheerful hotels with beach access for €60–140. The trick is understanding what you're paying for and what you're willing to trade.

## Luxury Caldera Hotels

### Katikies Hotel (Oia)

If there's one hotel that embodies the Santorini dream, it's Katikies. Carved into the caldera cliff in Oia, with infinity pools that seem to pour over the edge into the Aegean below, whitewashed cave suites, and a restaurant — Mikrasia — that serves some of the island's finest food. Every room faces the caldera. Every evening ends with the sunset. The service is impeccable — quiet, anticipatory, the kind that makes you feel like you're the only guest.

This is a once-in-a-lifetime hotel, and it delivers on that promise. The cave suites are naturally cool even in peak summer. The common areas are elegant without being stiff. And the sunset from the pool terrace is the Santorini moment that every visitor imagines before they arrive.

**Price range:** €500–1,500/night
**Best for:** Honeymooners, luxury travelers, anyone for whom Santorini is a dream trip
**Good to know:** Book months in advance for June–September. The cliffside layout means extensive steps; not suitable for guests with mobility issues. Mikrasia restaurant is worth booking even if you stay elsewhere.

[Check prices for Katikies Hotel on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/katikies.html)

### Canaves Oia Suites

Canaves takes a more contemporary approach than the traditional cave aesthetic — spacious suites with private plunge pools, crisp white interiors, and caldera views that stop conversation. The main pool area is one of the most photographed hotel spots on the island. Service is warm and professional. For couples who want luxury without the weight of tradition, Canaves is the modern answer.

The Canaves brand has expanded to several properties in Oia — Suites, Epitome, and Sunday. Suites is the original and best located for the village center. All share similar quality standards.

**Price range:** €450–1,200/night
**Best for:** Couples wanting modern luxury, honeymooners, design lovers
**Good to know:** The Oia Suites property is the best positioned. Book well ahead — Canaves is one of the most in-demand hotel brands in Greece.

[Check prices for Canaves Oia Suites on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/canaves-oia.html)

### Grace Hotel (Imerovigli)

One of the finest luxury hotels on Santorini, recently rebranded under the Auberge Resorts collection. The infinity pool — an elongated, architectural masterpiece cantilevered over the caldera — is one of the most photographed hotel pools in the world. Cave suites are minimalist and serene, with the kind of considered design where every detail has been thought through. The restaurant is excellent, the spa intimate, and the overall experience polished without being corporate.

Imerovigli is the highest point on the caldera rim, and Grace occupies its most commanding position. The views — sweeping across the entire caldera, the volcanic islands, and the sea beyond — are the most panoramic on the island.

**Price range:** €600–2,000/night
**Best for:** Peak Santorini luxury, design lovers, special celebrations
**Good to know:** This is the top tier. The price reflects it. But if you're going to splurge once in Greece, a night here with that pool and that view is an experience that justifies the expense. The Skaros Rock hike starts from the hotel's doorstep — do it at sunrise.

## Boutique & Design Hotels

### Astra Suites (Imerovigli)

Consistently ranked among the top hotels on Santorini by guests — and the gap between Astra's prices and the Oia mega-luxury properties makes it arguably the best value in the caldera-view category. Suites are carved into the cliff with private terraces and hot tubs or plunge pools. The pool area is stunning. Breakfast is excellent — served in your suite or on the terrace. The staff have the kind of attentive warmth that makes you feel genuinely cared for rather than processed.

Less famous than Grace or Katikies, which means slightly more accessible prices for comparable quality. This is the caldera hotel that people who know Santorini recommend to friends.

**Price range:** €350–900/night
**Best for:** Honeymooners wanting value-luxury, couples, travelers who prefer substance over brand name
**Good to know:** One of the best honeymoon hotels on the island. Private hot tubs in most suites. The caldera path to Fira takes about 25 minutes on foot — a beautiful evening walk.

[Check prices for Astra Suites on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/astra-apartments.html)

### Cosmopolitan Suites (Fira)

A luxury boutique carved into the caldera cliff at the quieter northern end of Fira, with suites featuring private hot tubs or plunge pools overlooking the crater. The design is contemporary Cycladic — clean white lines, natural stone, pops of blue — and the views from every room are extraordinary. The pool terrace is intimate, breakfast is excellent, and the whole property feels like a cocoon of calm above Fira's energy.

Fira gets less credit than Oia for its hotels, which creates a pricing advantage. Cosmopolitan delivers a caldera experience that rivals Oia properties at 30–40% lower rates, with the added convenience of Fira's restaurants, bars, and bus connections at your doorstep.

**Price range:** €300–700/night
**Best for:** Couples wanting caldera luxury without Oia prices, first-time visitors wanting the best of Fira
**Good to know:** The northern location puts you right on the caldera path toward Firostefani — a beautiful evening walk. Steps are inevitable; this hotel is no exception.

[Check prices for Cosmopolitan Suites on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/cosmopolitan-suites.html)

### Athina Luxury Suites (Fira)

A caldera-view hotel in central Fira with a rooftop pool and views that rival properties charging twice as much. Suites are well-designed with balconies or terraces facing the caldera. The location — walking distance from the bus station, restaurants, and the Fira-to-Oia path — is ideal for exploring without a car.

The value proposition is the strongest on the caldera side of the island. A rooftop pool, caldera views, Fira convenience, and prices that make the caldera accessible to travelers who don't have €500 a night to spend.

**Price range:** €200–500/night
**Best for:** Couples wanting caldera views at mid-range prices, first-time visitors, practical travelers
**Good to know:** One of the best value caldera-view hotels on the island. Central location means some town noise, but the views compensate.

[Check prices for Athina Luxury Suites on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/athina-luxury-suites.html)

## Mid-Range Caldera Hotels

### Nonis Apartments (Firostefani)

A family-run property in Firostefani with caldera-view apartments that offer extraordinary value. Studios and one-bedroom apartments have kitchenettes, terraces with volcano views, and the kind of genuine Greek hospitality that five-star hotels can't replicate. The location — steps from the famous blue-domed church (Agios Theodori, the one on most Santorini postcards) and a ten-minute walk to Fira — is superb.

Nonis represents what Santorini used to be before the luxury hotels arrived — a family offering honest rooms with an honest view at an honest price. The fact that the view from these terraces is essentially the same view that €800 hotels sell speaks to the democracy of Santorini's geography.

**Price range:** €100–250/night
**Best for:** Budget-conscious couples wanting caldera views, families, self-caterers, longer stays
**Good to know:** Book the caldera-facing units specifically — not all apartments face the volcano. The kitchenette is a genuine money-saver on an island where restaurant prices can be punishing. Stock up at a Fira supermarket.

[Check prices for Nonis Apartments on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/nonis-apartments.html)

### Villa Renos (Fira)

The best budget caldera-view option in Fira. A family-run hotel with clean, simple rooms, a terrace overlooking the volcano, and prices that make the caldera accessible to travelers who don't have five-star budgets. The view from the breakfast terrace is genuine caldera magic — the same Aegean, the same volcanic islands, the same sky — at a fraction of what the boutiques charge.

The family who runs it is warm and helpful, the kind of hosts who make you feel like a guest rather than a transaction.

**Price range:** €80–180/night
**Best for:** Budget travelers wanting a caldera view, solo visitors, anyone who values authentic hospitality
**Good to know:** Extremely popular — book well ahead. Caldera-view rooms are the ones you want; non-view rooms are cheaper but miss the point. Breakfast on the terrace is the highlight.

[Check prices for Villa Renos on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/villa-renos.html)

### Oia's Sunset Apartments (Oia)

Proof that you can stay in Oia without liquidating your savings. These apartments are simple, clean, and well-maintained, with kitchenettes and — crucially — some units with caldera views at prices that would buy you a pillow at Katikies. The location is on the quieter southern end of Oia, away from the worst of the day-tripper crowds.

**Price range:** €120–280/night
**Best for:** Budget travelers who want to say they stayed in Oia, self-caterers, practical romantics
**Good to know:** Book the caldera-view units specifically. The kitchenette saves money. Oia's mini-markets are limited; stock up at a Fira supermarket.

[Check prices for Oia's Sunset Apartments on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/oia-sunset-apartments.html)

## East Coast & Beach Hotels

The east coast is where Santorini becomes affordable — and where it becomes a different kind of holiday entirely. No caldera views, but black-sand beaches, taverna culture, and the kind of relaxed seaside atmosphere that the caldera villages lost long ago.

### Anassa Deluxe Suites (Kamari)

A modern boutique hotel near Kamari Beach with clean, contemporary rooms, a pool, and a level of design quality that would cost three times as much on the caldera side. Some suites have private hot tubs. The beach is a five-minute walk. Breakfast is included and good.

Kamari's bus to Fira runs frequently (about fifteen minutes), which means you can visit the caldera villages for sunset and return to Kamari for an affordable dinner. The savings on accommodation fund the experiences — a caldera catamaran cruise, a wine tour, a dinner at Argo in Fira.

**Price range:** €120–280/night
**Best for:** Families, budget-smart travelers, beach lovers, anyone who prefers value over views
**Good to know:** No caldera view — this is the east coast. But the savings allow splurges elsewhere. The headland above Kamari holds Ancient Thera, a well-preserved archaeological site with exceptional views of both coasts.

[Check prices for Anassa Deluxe Suites on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/anassa-deluxe-suites.html)

### Hotel Matina (Kamari)

A family-run gem near the center of Kamari with a beautiful garden, a pool, and rooms that are clean, cheerful, and comfortable. The family goes out of their way to help — restaurant recommendations, bus timetables, day-trip advice — and the breakfast, served in the garden, features homemade jams and fresh-baked bread. This is Santorini the way it used to be, before the caldera became the world's most expensive selfie backdrop.

**Price range:** €60–140/night
**Best for:** [Families](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/best-greek-islands-for-families), budget travelers, anyone who values warmth and honesty over luxury
**Good to know:** Genuinely affordable for Santorini. The garden and pool area are the highlights. The beach is a short walk. This is the island's best-kept accommodation secret.

[Check prices for Hotel Matina on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/matina.html)

## The Pyrgos Alternative

### Zannos Melathron (Pyrgos)

Most visitors drive through Pyrgos without stopping. Their loss. This medieval hilltop village — the highest on the island — has 360-degree views, almost no tourists, and Zannos Melathron: a neoclassical mansion converted into a boutique hotel with a pool, terrace views, and interiors that blend historical elegance with modern comfort. The village's castle ruin offers the single best panoramic view on Santorini — the entire caldera, both coasts, the mountains.

Selene, one of the island's most celebrated restaurants, is in Pyrgos. Franco's Café, just below the castle, serves drinks with what may be the best view on the island. This is Santorini for travelers who've already done Oia and want something deeper.

**Price range:** €200–500/night
**Best for:** Returning visitors, food lovers, photographers, anyone wanting quiet and authenticity
**Good to know:** Pyrgos is central on the island — a good base for wine tours and exploring in all directions. No caldera cliff drama; the appeal is village atmosphere and elevated panoramic views. A car is useful from Pyrgos.

## Practical Tips for Santorini Hotels

**Book early.** The best caldera hotels sell out 3–6 months ahead for peak season. Even shoulder-season bookings for top properties need 2–3 months.

**Caldera vs east coast economics.** A caldera-view room averages €300–500 in peak season. An east-coast hotel averages €80–180. The caldera view is extraordinary, but staying on the east coast and busing to Fira for sunset saves hundreds per night. Both are valid strategies.

**The wine experience.** Santorini's volcanic soil produces world-class Assyrtiko white wine. Santo Wines, Venetsanos, and Gavalas are all worth visiting. A wine tour is one of the most rewarding things you can do on the island — and one of the few activities that feels underpriced.

**The caldera cruise.** A half-day catamaran or sailing cruise — stopping at the volcanic hot springs, swimming in the caldera, watching the sunset from the water — is the essential Santorini activity.
