## ARTICLE BODY

# Best Hotels in Samos, Greece: Our Top Picks for 2026

Samos is the island of Pythagoras, of Hera, of wine — and of a landscape so green and mountainous that arriving from the dry Cyclades feels like landing in a different climate zone entirely. The pine forests climb to peaks over 1,400 meters (Mount Kerkis, the highest in the Aegean islands). The vineyards produce Muscat wine that has been celebrated since antiquity — Homer mentioned it, and the sweet, amber-colored Samos Muscat remains one of the most distinctive wines in Greece. The orchards grow olives, citrus, and stone fruit. And the coast alternates between pebble coves, sandy beaches, and fishing harbors where the afternoon catch still determines the evening menu.

The island sits tantalizingly close to Turkey — at the narrowest point of the strait, the Turkish coast is barely a kilometer away, and the lights of Kusadasi are visible from the eastern shore at night. This proximity has shaped Samos's history: the ancient city-state was one of the most powerful in the Greek world, the Temple of Hera (the Heraion) was one of the most ambitious architectural projects of antiquity, and the Eupalinian Tunnel — a kilometer-long aqueduct driven through solid rock in the 6th century BC — remains one of the greatest engineering achievements of the ancient world. Both are UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

The hotel scene reflects the island's character: cultured, agricultural, and improving year by year. No mega-resorts, but a growing number of properties with genuine design ambition and the local products — wine, olive oil, honey — to back it up.

## Boutique & Design Hotels

### Ino Village Hotel (Vathy area)

The most complete hotel on Samos — a hillside property above Vathy with a pool, well-designed bungalow-style rooms set among gardens and olive trees, a restaurant serving creative Greek cuisine, and views across the deep harbor to the mountains beyond. The architecture blends traditional Samian stone with contemporary comfort, and the garden setting creates the feel of a small village rather than a conventional hotel.

The restaurant makes genuine use of local products — Samos wine, of course, but also olive oil from the island's groves, honey from the mountain beekeepers, and vegetables from nearby producers. The breakfast is a highlight.

**Price range:** €120–280/night
**Best for:** Couples, wine lovers, anyone wanting the island's most refined property
**Good to know:** The hillside location means a short drive to Vathy center and the harbor. The pool and gardens are the hotel's social center. A car is essential for the beaches and Pythagorio.

### Proteas Blu Resort

A beachfront resort on the south coast near Pythagorio with a spa, pool, modern rooms, and an adults-only section that provides a quieter atmosphere. The proximity to the Samos wine country — the vineyards are concentrated in the south — gives wine-focused travelers easy access to tastings and cellars. The beach is pebbly but the water is clear and warm.

**Price range:** €150–350/night
**Best for:** Couples, wine enthusiasts, adults-only seekers, anyone wanting south-coast luxury
**Good to know:** Adults-only section is the quieter option. Pythagorio is about 15 minutes by car. The Heraion (Temple of Hera) is nearby. The south coast is warmer and calmer than the wind-exposed north.

## Kokkari Hotels

Kokkari is the village that appears on every Samos photograph — a small fishing harbor on the north coast where stone houses line a waterfront with two small beaches, tavernas serve fresh fish on tables that extend to the water's edge, and the mountain behind rises steeply, covered in pine forest. It's the island's most charming village and its most popular tourist base.

### Athena Hotel (Kokkari)

A waterfront hotel in the heart of Kokkari with clean, updated rooms, some with balconies overlooking the harbor and the sea. The location is the main draw — step outside and you're on the waterfront, with the fishing boats, the tavernas, and the small beaches all within a one-minute walk. The hotel has been well-maintained and the rooms recently refreshed.

**Price range:** €80–180/night
**Best for:** Couples, village-atmosphere seekers, first-time Samos visitors wanting Kokkari's best location
**Good to know:** Kokkari is small — a handful of tavernas, a couple of minimarkets, and a genuine fishing-village atmosphere. The north coast catches more wind than the south — windsurfers love it, calm-water swimmers should head to the sheltered coves. Vathy is about 15 minutes by car.

[Check prices for Athena Hotel on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/athena-kokkari.html)

### Pension Dreams (Kokkari)

A family-run pension in Kokkari with simple, clean rooms and the kind of genuine Greek hospitality that larger hotels study but can't replicate. The family knows every beach on the island, every taverna worth visiting, and every vineyard that welcomes visitors. The rooms are basic. The warmth is exceptional.

**Price range:** €40–90/night
**Best for:** Budget travelers, solo visitors, young couples, anyone who values human warmth over hotel amenities
**Good to know:** Basic rooms — manage expectations. No pool, no restaurant. Kokkari's waterfront tavernas are the dining room. The value proposition — Kokkari's atmosphere at pension prices — is strong.

## Pythagorio & South Coast

### Doryssa Seaside Resort (Pythagorio)

The largest resort on Samos — set on a beach near the UNESCO-listed town of Pythagorio, with a pool, a spa, multiple restaurants, and comprehensive resort facilities. The property includes a "traditional village" section — rooms styled as a Samian village, with stone houses arranged around lanes and squares. The concept is unusual and adds character to what would otherwise be a standard beach resort.

Pythagorio itself is a small, attractive harbor town built over the ancient city of Samos — the birthplace of Pythagoras. The Eupalinian Tunnel and the foundations of the ancient harbor walls are nearby. The town's waterfront restaurants are pleasant, and the evening volta along the harbor is the nightly social ritual.

**Price range:** €120–300/night
**Best for:** Families, resort seekers, history enthusiasts wanting proximity to Pythagorio's UNESCO sites
**Good to know:** Large resort — can feel impersonal at peak occupancy. The "traditional village" rooms are more characterful than the main building. Pythagorio's harbor is a short walk. The Heraion is about 10 minutes by car.

[Check prices for Doryssa Seaside Resort on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/doryssa-seaside.html)

## Practical Tips for Samos Hotels

**Getting there.** Samos has an airport (SMI) with flights from Athens (about 1 hour, daily) and seasonal connections from Thessaloniki and European cities. Ferries from Piraeus take about 12 hours (overnight). Samos connects to [Ikaria](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/ikaria-travel-guide) (1 hour by ferry), [Patmos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/patmos-travel-guide) (~2 hours), and other Dodecanese and northeast Aegean islands. A boat to Kusadasi, Turkey (for Ephesus day trips) runs seasonally from Vathy.

**A rental car is essential.** Samos is large (about 40 km long) and mountainous. The three main bases (Vathy, Kokkari, Pythagorio) are spread across the island, and the best beaches, vineyards, and villages require driving. Roads are well-paved on main routes.

**The wine.** Samos Muscat is famous worldwide — a sweet, amber wine produced from Muscat grapes grown on terraced hillsides. Visit the Samos Wine Museum in Vathy or the cooperative's tasting room. The vineyard landscape on the south-facing slopes is beautiful.

**Three bases.** Vathy for practicality (port, restaurants, museum), Kokkari for charm (fishing village, waterfront tavernas, north-coast beaches), Pythagorio for history (UNESCO sites, ancient harbor, south-coast beaches). They're all within 20–30 minutes of each other.

**When to visit.** May–June and September–October. Samos has a longer growing season than the Cyclades, and the greenery is most impressive in spring. July–August are hot and the north coast gets windy (good for windsurfing, challenging for some swimmers). See our [Greece weather guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/greece-weather-by-month).

**Combining with other islands.** Samos pairs naturally with [Ikaria](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/ikaria-travel-guide) (just 1 hour by ferry — the easiest combination), [Patmos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/patmos-travel-guide), and [Chios](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/chios-travel-guide). The Turkey crossing to Kusadasi for an Ephesus day trip is a unique option that no other Greek island offers this easily.
