# Best Hotels in Elounda, Crete: Our Top Picks for 2026

Elounda is where Greece does luxury at its most serious. Not the bottle-service, look-at-me luxury of Mykonos or the caldera-view markup of Santorini — something quieter, more established, and more focused on the experience itself. The resorts here have been refined over decades by families and operators who understand that genuine luxury is not about gold taps and marble lobbies. It's about a private pool that catches the morning light on Mirabello Bay. A breakfast where the honey comes from the mountain behind the hotel. A staff member who remembers that you take your coffee without sugar and that your anniversary is tomorrow.

The setting helps. [Elounda](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/elounda-travel-guide) occupies a stretch of eastern [Crete](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/trip-to-crete-greece)'s coast where the mountains descend to a calm, sheltered bay and the island of Spinalonga — once a Venetian fortress, then Greece's last leper colony, now one of the most visited archaeological sites in Crete — adds both visual drama and historical gravity to every sea-facing terrace. The water is warm, clear, and calm — the bay is protected from the open Aegean — and the microclimate is reliably sunny from May through October.

This is not a budget destination. Elounda's luxury corridor starts at roughly €250 per night and the top suites can exceed €2,000. But for the right occasion — a [honeymoon](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/best-greek-islands-for-couples), an anniversary, a milestone trip — the investment buys an experience that stands alongside the finest resort hotels anywhere in the world.

For the broader Crete picture, read our [where to stay in Crete](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-crete) and [best hotels in Crete](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/best-hotels-in-crete) guides. For the nearby town alternative, see our [best hotels in Agios Nikolaos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/best-hotels-in-agios-nikolaos). This article focuses on Elounda itself.

## World-Class Luxury Resorts

### Blue Palace Resort & Spa

The flagship of Elounda and the hotel against which every other luxury resort in Greece is measured. Blue Palace sits on a hillside facing Spinalonga, with rooms and suites terraced down toward a private beach and the bay. Most suites have private plunge pools or infinity pools — the kind that seem to pour directly into the Aegean below. The architecture is contemporary Cretan: stone, earth tones, low-rise volumes integrated into the landscape.

The spa is among the finest in Greece. The restaurants — multiple venues covering Greek, Mediterranean, and fine dining — operate at a standard that would hold its own in any major city. The private beach is immaculate. And the service is the quality that separates great hotels from merely expensive ones: attentive without being intrusive, personal without being presumptuous, the kind of hospitality that makes you feel simultaneously pampered and at home.

Blue Palace is the hotel for travelers who have experienced luxury before and know what genuine quality looks like. It doesn't need to perform — it simply delivers, consistently and quietly.

**Price range:** €300–1,200/night
**Best for:** Honeymooners, anniversary celebrations, luxury seekers, anyone for whom the hotel is a destination in itself
**Good to know:** The hillside layout means walking or buggy rides between facilities. Half-board packages often represent better value than room-only. The Spinalonga boat dock is nearby — take the morning trip before the crowds. Book 4–6 months ahead for July–August.

[Check prices for Blue Palace on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/blue-palace-resort-spa.html)

### Elounda Mare Hotel

A Relais & Châteaux property that represents a different register of luxury than Blue Palace — more intimate, more traditionally elegant, and more quietly refined. Elounda Mare's bungalows are set in landscaped gardens that descend to a private beach, many with private pools. The atmosphere is understated and personal: old-school Mediterranean luxury rather than contemporary design, delivered with the kind of confidence that comes from being excellent for a very long time.

The restaurant serves Greek-Mediterranean cuisine of genuine quality. The beach is private and immaculate. The spa is intimate rather than expansive. Everything operates with the quiet assurance of a property that doesn't need to shout about what it is.

The bungalow-with-private-pool experience is transformative. The privacy is complete — your own terrace, your own pool, the sea beyond, and no reason to leave except hunger or curiosity. For couples celebrating something important, this level of seclusion is priceless.

**Price range:** €280–900/night
**Best for:** Couples seeking privacy and refinement, repeat Elounda visitors, travelers who prefer understated elegance to contemporary flash
**Good to know:** Smaller and more intimate than Blue Palace, which is either an advantage or a limitation depending on preferences. The Relais & Châteaux standards ensure consistency. Fewer facilities than the larger resorts — this is about quality, not quantity.

[Check prices for Elounda Mare on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/elounda-mare.html)

### Elounda Bay Palace

The Elounda resort that families love most. The grounds are manicured and extensive. The beach is private, sandy, and well-serviced. The kids' club, the family pools, the activities program — all are genuine rather than tokenistic, staffed by people who understand that a family holiday succeeds or fails on whether the children are happy.

Rooms and suites are spacious — family suites have genuine living space, not the cramped afterthought that some luxury hotels call "family accommodation." The restaurants cover enough cuisines and formats to satisfy different appetites across a week-long stay. The all-inclusive option eliminates the financial anxiety that large-family dining creates.

**Price range:** €250–800/night
**Best for:** Families with children wanting five-star comfort, upscale family holidays, multi-generational trips
**Good to know:** The resort is large — check room location at booking, as some buildings are further from the beach than others. The all-inclusive option is worth calculating versus room-only plus dining. The calm, warm Mirabello Bay water is ideal for young swimmers.

[Check prices for Elounda Bay Palace on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/elounda-bay-palace.html)

### Domes of Elounda

The most contemporary design sensibility on the Elounda coast — a resort that blends Cycladic minimalism with Cretan warmth in a way that feels both current and rooted. Suites have private pools, terraces face the bay, and the adults-only section (Domes Haute Living) provides an intimate enclave within the larger property. The spa is excellent. The restaurants serve creative Greek-Mediterranean cuisine.

Domes appeals to the design-conscious luxury traveler — someone who wants the Elounda setting and service but in a more modern architectural language than the established resorts. The brand's aesthetic is confident: white volumes, natural stone, careful lighting, and spaces designed to frame the landscape rather than compete with it.

**Price range:** €350–1,000/night
**Best for:** Design lovers, younger luxury travelers, couples wanting a contemporary take on Elounda luxury
**Good to know:** The adults-only section (Haute Living) is the best part for couples without children. The main resort area accommodates families. The design is deliberately minimal — opulence lovers may prefer Blue Palace's more traditional approach.

[Check prices for Domes of Elounda on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/domes-of-elounda.html)

## Boutique & Mid-Range Hotels

### Elounda Ilion

A smaller-scale alternative to the mega-resorts — a boutique hotel on the hillside above the bay with a pool, sea views, and the kind of personal service that only intimate properties can provide. Rooms are well-designed in a contemporary Cretan style. The breakfast is homemade and uses local products. The atmosphere is warm and unhurried.

Elounda Ilion works for travelers who want the Elounda setting — the bay, the mountains, the light — without the resort-scale experience. It's the kind of hotel where the owner greets you, the staff know your preferences by day two, and the scale allows genuine care rather than systematic hospitality.

**Price range:** €130–280/night
**Best for:** Couples wanting boutique intimacy, budget-conscious luxury seekers, travelers who prefer small hotels
**Good to know:** No private beach — the village's small beach and swimming areas are nearby. The pool with bay views is the property's social center. A car is needed for exploring eastern Crete.

[Check prices for Elounda Ilion on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/elounda-ilion.html)

### Elounda Orama

A mid-range hotel near the village with a pool terrace overlooking Mirabello Bay, clean and comfortable rooms, and prices that make Elounda accessible to travelers who don't have resort-level budgets. The village tavernas are walking distance. The views from the pool — across the bay to Spinalonga and the mountains — would cost five times as much from a resort terrace.

**Price range:** €90–200/night
**Best for:** Budget-conscious visitors who still want the Elounda setting, couples, practical travelers
**Good to know:** No private beach, no spa, no resort facilities. The pool and view are the amenities. The village is walkable. [Agios Nikolaos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/agios-nikolaos-travel-guide) is 15 minutes by car for more dining and shopping options.

[Check prices for Elounda Orama on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/elounda-orama.html)

### Elounda Apartments

Self-catering studios and apartments in the village — basic, clean, and functional, with kitchenettes and enough space to feel like a temporary home. The price is genuinely affordable for Elounda, which makes the location — walking distance to the waterfront, the Spinalonga boats, and the tavernas — feel like a steal.

**Price range:** €50–120/night
**Best for:** Budget travelers, self-caterers, families wanting independence, longer stays
**Good to know:** No pool, no resort facilities. The village itself is your base. The kitchenette saves significant money on an expensive coast. A car is essential for beaches and wider exploration.

## Practical Tips for Elounda Hotels

**Spinalonga.** The island-fortress across the bay is one of the most compelling sites in Crete. Boats depart from Elounda village (10 minutes), from Plaka (5 minutes, more scenic), and from [Agios Nikolaos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/agios-nikolaos-travel-guide) harbor (30 minutes). Go in the morning to avoid the midday heat and the tour-bus crowds. The history — Venetian fort, Ottoman garrison, leper colony — is moving and well-presented.

**When to visit.** May–June and September–October. Eastern Crete is drier and warmer than the west, with comfortable swimming into late October. July–August are very hot but the Mirabello Bay breezes help. The luxury resorts are busiest (and priciest) in July–August — shoulder months offer the same weather at 30–40% lower rates. See our [Greece weather guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/greece-weather-by-month).

**Getting there.** Fly into Heraklion airport (HER), about 65 km / 1 hour drive east along the north-coast highway. The [Chania airport](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/chania-travel-guide) (CHQ) is 3+ hours — use Heraklion. Most resorts arrange private transfers. A rental car is useful for day trips but not essential if you're staying at a self-contained resort.

**Exploring eastern Crete.** From Elounda: Agios Nikolaos (15 min), Lasithi Plateau (45 min), Vai Palm Beach (90 min), [Sitia](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/sitia-travel-guide) (60 min), [Heraklion](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/heraklion-travel-guide) and Knossos (60 min). See our [Greece road trip guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/greece-road-trip).

**Is it worth the money?** If you can afford it and your trip warrants it — absolutely. The Elounda resorts deliver a caliber of hospitality, setting, and privacy that ranks with the finest anywhere in the Mediterranean. For a honeymoon, anniversary, or milestone celebration, the investment creates memories that justify the cost. For a casual beach holiday, the same money goes further at [Agios Nikolaos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/best-hotels-in-agios-nikolaos), [Rethymno](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/rethymno-travel-guide), or the Cyclades.
