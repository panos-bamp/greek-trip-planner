# Best Restaurants in Ios, Greece: Where to Eat in 2026

Ios is the Cycladic island in the middle of a reinvention — and the food scene is the clearest evidence. A decade ago, eating on Ios meant souvlaki at 3 AM or a beach burger between swims. The island's reputation as a party destination for twenty-somethings left little room for culinary ambition, and the restaurants reflected this: functional, cheap, and designed to soak up the previous night's excess rather than create a new evening's pleasure.

That Ios still exists — the party lanes in the Chora are alive and well — but it now shares the island with something different. Creative restaurants have opened in the Chora's quieter lanes, run by chefs who see the island's Cycladic ingredients, its spectacular setting, and its lower costs as an opportunity to cook seriously at prices that the more famous islands can no longer offer. Wine bars have appeared alongside the shot bars. Honest tavernas have improved their sourcing and cooking. And the overall dining experience has elevated to a level that makes Ios a genuine food destination, not just a party one.

## Chora (Hilltop Village)

### Grandma's

The restaurant that changed how people think about eating on Ios — a creative Greek kitchen in the Chora's lanes that takes Cycladic ingredients and treats them with genuine culinary care. The name is misleading: this is not grandmother's cooking in the sentimental sense, but a modern kitchen that uses the flavors of traditional Greek cooking (the herbs, the slow braises, the fresh fish) as a starting point for dishes that are precise, well-plated, and occasionally surprising.

The setting — a terrace in the Chora's lanes with views to the sea — provides the atmosphere that Ios's hilltop position makes available to every restaurant with a terrace and the intelligence to face it toward the sunset.

**Cuisine:** Creative Cycladic-Greek
**Price range:** €25–40/person
**Best for:** Couples, food-curious travelers, anyone wanting Ios's most ambitious cooking
**Good to know:** Reserve for dinner — the best tables fill early. The terrace is the preferred seating. The menu changes with the season and the market. The wine list features Cycladic producers worth exploring.

### Lord Byron

A Chora taverna that has been the reliable constant through Ios's party decades and its food evolution — serving honest Greek cooking (grilled meats, fresh fish, meze, salads) with the consistency that travelers depend on when they've been burned by too many tourist-strip restaurants. The name is an eccentric tribute, the setting is pleasant (a terrace in the quieter lanes), and the cooking is straightforward and well-executed.

Lord Byron is the restaurant you go to when you want to eat well without adventure — when the Greek salad should be perfect, the fish should be grilled correctly, and the bill should be fair. It delivers on all three, reliably.

**Cuisine:** Traditional Greek
**Price range:** €15–28/person
**Best for:** Practical travelers, families, anyone wanting reliable quality in the Chora
**Good to know:** The terrace is pleasant and relatively quiet (for Ios). Open for lunch and dinner. The fish and the lamb chops are the strongest orders. No need to reserve for lunch; dinner reservations help in peak season.

### Pathos Sunset Lounge

A sunset-terrace restaurant-bar on the western edge of the Chora with views across the Aegean that would command €80 per person on [Santorini](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/best-restaurants-in-santorini) and cost about half that on Ios. The food is Greek-Mediterranean — meze, seafood, creative plates — served alongside cocktails and a wine list, in a setting designed for the golden hour. The cooking is good without being the main event; the sunset is the headliner.

**Cuisine:** Greek-Mediterranean, sunset bar-restaurant
**Price range:** €20–35/person
**Best for:** Sunset seekers, couples, the Ios caldera-equivalent experience at Ios prices
**Good to know:** Arrive an hour before sunset for the best tables. The cocktails are well-made. The food is the supporting act — enjoy it alongside the view. The atmosphere transitions from dining to bar as the evening progresses.

### Katogi (Chora)

A wine bar and small-plates restaurant in the Chora that captures Ios's evolving evening culture — the transition from shots to sipping, from noise to conversation. The wine list features Cycladic and Greek producers, the plates are designed for sharing, and the atmosphere is convivial without being rowdy.

**Cuisine:** Wine bar, Greek small plates
**Price range:** €18–30/person
**Best for:** Wine lovers, couples, the quieter Ios evening
**Good to know:** The wine list is the draw — ask the staff to guide you. Small plates work better than ordering mains. The lane-setting is atmospheric. Later in the evening it gets livelier.

### Thai Ios

An unexpected addition to the Chora — a Thai restaurant that serves genuinely well-executed Thai food in a Cycladic setting. The curries are properly spiced, the pad thai is competent, and the departure from Greek food provides a welcome change after several days of taverna dining. It shouldn't work on a Cycladic island, and it does.

**Cuisine:** Thai
**Price range:** €15–25/person
**Best for:** Anyone wanting a break from Greek food, spice lovers, the unexpectedly good detour
**Good to know:** The quality is genuine — the chef has Thai training. The portion sizes are fair. It's a change of pace, not a replacement for the Greek dining — alternate evenings.

## Mylopotas Beach

### Drakos Taverna

A beachfront taverna on Mylopotas — the island's main beach — with tables on the sand and a menu of Greek taverna standards: grilled fish, calamari, meze, salads, and the cold beer that a hot Cycladic afternoon demands. Drakos is the honest beach taverna: not ambitious, not trying to be Instagram-worthy, just fresh food at fair prices with the sand between your toes.

**Cuisine:** Greek beach taverna
**Price range:** €12–22/person
**Best for:** Beach-day lunches, families, feet-in-the-sand casual eating
**Good to know:** Mylopotas is about a 15-minute walk or short bus ride from the Chora. The beach has organized and free sections. Drakos is at the calmer end. The fish is fresh and the salads are honest.

### Octopus Tree

On the road between the port and Mylopotas — a seafood restaurant whose name doubles as its best recommendation. The grilled octopus is excellent, the fish is fresh, and the meze plates cover the Cycladic seafood range. The setting is roadside rather than beachfront — less photogenic but more focused on the food.

**Cuisine:** Seafood, meze
**Price range:** €18–30/person
**Best for:** Seafood lovers, the island's best octopus, a non-beach seafood dinner
**Good to know:** The roadside location means no sea view — you're eating for the fish, not the setting. The octopus (grilled, marinated, or in salad) is the signature. Reserve for dinner in peak season.

## Port (Gialos)

### Ios Club (port area, sunset terrace)

A cocktail bar and restaurant near the port with a terrace that catches the sunset — less crowded than the Chora's sunset spots and with a more relaxed, less party-oriented atmosphere. The food is decent Greek-Mediterranean, the cocktails are well-made, and the position provides one of the island's better sunset views without the hilltop climb.

**Cuisine:** Greek-Mediterranean, cocktail bar
**Price range:** €15–30/person
**Best for:** Sunset drinks, arriving visitors wanting a first-evening base, relaxed atmosphere
**Good to know:** Less frenetic than the Chora bars. Good for an early-evening drink and a light meal before heading up to the Chora for dinner.

## Budget & Late Night

### Chora Souvlaki Joints

Ios's souvlaki culture serves a dual purpose: budget fuel for daytime visitors and 3 AM sustenance for the nightlife crowd. Multiple souvlaki shops line the Chora's main lane, and the quality is uniformly decent — handmade pita, well-seasoned meat, fresh toppings. At €4–6 per wrap, they remain the best-value meal on an island where even the tavernas are already affordable.

**Cuisine:** Souvlaki, gyros
**Price range:** €4–6/wrap
**Best for:** Budget travelers, late-night fuel, the Ios rite of passage
**Good to know:** Multiple options — wander and choose. Cash preferred. The late-night hours (past 2 AM) are the souvlaki shops' peak business. The quality difference between shops is minimal.

## Practical Tips for Eating in Ios

**Chora vs Mylopotas.** The Chora for the best restaurants — creative Greek, tavernas, wine bars, sunset terraces. Mylopotas for beach lunches and casual beachfront dining. They're connected by a 15-minute walk or a short bus ride. Dinner should be in the Chora; lunch can be on the beach.

**The food evolution.** Ios's food scene has genuinely improved — but it hasn't fully shed the party-island infrastructure. The main party lane in the Chora still has cheap-drink bars and late-night snack joints alongside the new wine bars and creative restaurants. The two worlds coexist, and the key is knowing which lane to walk down.

**When to eat.** Lunch: 1–3 PM (beach tavernas). Dinner: 8:30 PM onward (Chora). Sunset drinks: arrive by 7 PM at Pathos or Ios Club. Late-night souvlaki: midnight–3 AM. Ios eats late and stays up later.

**Value comparison.** A creative dinner on Ios costs roughly what a taverna meal costs on Santorini or Mykonos. The quality gap has closed; the price gap is your advantage. The island's lower profile keeps everything more honest.
