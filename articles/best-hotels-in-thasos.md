## Key Takeaways

1. Thasos is northern Greece's island gem — densely forested with pine, olive, and plane trees, ringed by beaches that range from golden sand to white marble pebble, and close enough to the mainland (a 35-minute ferry from Keramoti) to make it the easiest island to reach in northern Greece.
2. The beaches are extraordinary and uncrowded. Golden Beach and Paradise Beach on the east coast have long stretches of fine sand and clear water. Marble Beach (Saliara) on the northeast is a cove of white marble pebbles and turquoise water that looks like it belongs in the Caribbean. And they're all significantly emptier than comparable beaches in the Cyclades or Dodecanese.
3. Thasos has genuine ancient ruins — the ancient agora in Limenas (Thasos Town), the ancient theatre above the town, and the archaeological museum make this one of the most historically interesting islands outside Crete and Rhodes. The island was famous in antiquity for its gold and silver mines, its wine, and its marble.
4. The island is popular with Greek families, Bulgarians, Romanians, and northern Europeans — a different tourist mix from the American-and-British-dominated Cyclades. This gives the island a distinctly mainland-Greek and Balkan character, with prices that reflect a domestic rather than international market.
5. A rental car is essential — the island is about 26 km across, the beaches are scattered around the coast, and the mountain villages in the interior reward exploration.

# Best Hotels in Thasos, Greece: Our Top Picks for 2026

Thasos is the Greek island that northern Greece keeps for itself. While the international tourism machine directs travelers to [Santorini](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-santorini), [Mykonos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-mykonos), and [Crete](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-crete), Greek families from [Thessaloniki](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/thessaloniki-travel-guide) and Kavala board the short ferry to Thasos for beaches that are genuinely world-class, forests that are genuinely green, and prices that are genuinely affordable. The island has somehow avoided the international spotlight, and the result is a place where the beaches are uncrowded, the restaurants serve food for locals rather than tourists, and the atmosphere remains authentically Greek.

The landscape is lush by Aegean standards — pine forests blanket the mountainous interior, olive groves carpet the lower slopes, and the coast alternates between golden-sand beaches, hidden coves, and one extraordinary stretch of white marble pebble (Marble Beach / Saliara) where the stone glows against the turquoise water like a geological accident.

Limenas (Thasos Town), the capital, adds a historical dimension: the ancient agora, visible from the harbor, the remains of the ancient theatre on the hillside above, and an archaeological museum housing artifacts from the island's prosperous antiquity — when Thasos's gold mines, marble quarries, and vineyards made it one of the wealthiest islands in the ancient Greek world.

## Beach Hotels

### Makryammos Bungalows (Limenas area)

Bungalows set in a pine forest on a private cove just outside Limenas — combining forest shade, organized beach, and proximity to the capital's restaurants and ancient sites. The setting is beautiful: pine trees growing to the waterline, a small sandy beach, and the Aegean stretching north. The bungalow layout provides privacy and a village-like atmosphere.

**Price range:** €100–220/night
**Best for:** Couples, nature lovers, anyone wanting forest-and-beach near Limenas
**Good to know:** The pine-forest setting creates natural shade. The private cove is small but beautiful. Limenas center is about 5 minutes by car. The bungalows vary in size and condition — request recently updated ones.

### Alexandra Beach Resort (Potos)

A beachfront resort on the south coast near Potos with sandy beach access, pools, kids' facilities, and the comprehensive family infrastructure that makes week-long stays functional. Potos is the liveliest south-coast settlement — a waterfront village with tavernas, shops, and a sandy beach.

**Price range:** €100–250/night
**Best for:** Families with children, all-inclusive seekers, south-coast base
**Good to know:** Potos is about 25 minutes from Limenas. The south coast is warmer and calmer than the exposed east. The beach is sandy and organized. The resort's kids' facilities are genuine.

A beachfront resort on the west coast with a pool, comfortable rooms, and a mid-range proposition that delivers beach-holiday comfort at honest prices. The beach at Agios Ioannis is sandy and pleasant, with views toward the mainland and sunset light.

**Best for:** Mid-range seekers, couples, families wanting a west-coast beach base

## Practical Tips for Thasos Hotels

**Getting there.** Ferry from Keramoti (mainland): about 35 minutes — one of the shortest island ferry crossings in Greece, running every 30–60 minutes in summer. Keramoti is about 20 minutes from Kavala and about 2 hours from [Thessaloniki](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/thessaloniki-travel-guide) airport. A ferry also runs from Kavala to Skala Prinou on Thasos's west coast (about 1.5 hours).

**A rental car is essential.** The island is about 26 km across, and the best beaches are scattered around the coast. The coast road loops the island in about 90 minutes without stops. Roads are well-paved.

**The beaches.** Golden Beach and Paradise Beach (east coast) for long sandy stretches. Marble Beach / Saliara (northeast) for the surreal white-pebble-and-turquoise-water experience. Giola (south) is a natural rock pool — a lagoon-like formation where the sea fills a rocky hollow. Aliki (south) has ancient ruins next to the beach. All are significantly less crowded than comparable beaches in the Cyclades.

**When to visit.** June–September for beach weather. Thasos's season starts later and ends earlier than the southern islands — May and October are pleasant but the water is cooler. July–August are busy with Greek and Balkan holidaymakers. See our [Greece weather guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/greece-weather-by-month).

**Combining with mainland.** [Thessaloniki](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/thessaloniki-travel-guide) (2 hours from the ferry) and [Kavala](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/kavala-travel-guide) (20 minutes) are natural mainland additions.
