# Best Hotels in Parga, Greece: Our Top Picks for 2026

Parga is the kind of place that makes you question why you've been queueing for island ferries. The town tumbles down a pine-covered hillside to a harbor that curves like a cupped hand, its waterfront lined with tavernas whose tables extend to the water's edge. Above, a Venetian castle sits on a headland, its walls catching the last light of the day. Below, the water is Ionian turquoise — the same color that makes [Corfu](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-corfu) and [Lefkada](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/lefkada-travel-guide) famous, but without the boat ride.

The houses are painted in the warm pastels that Venetian influence left across the Ionian coast — terracotta, ochre, soft pink, faded blue — and from the castle above, the rooftop view looks like a Cézanne watercolor. The lanes are narrow and stepped, threading between the houses to emerge suddenly at viewpoints that stop you in your tracks. And the beaches — Krioneri at the town's feet, Valtos around the headland, Lichnos a short drive south — have the clear, warm water that most people associate exclusively with the islands.

Parga's secret is location. It sits on the Epirus coast, the least-touristed region of mainland Greece, where the mountains of the interior (the Zagori villages, Vikos Gorge, [Ioannina](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/ioannina-travel-guide)) descend to the Ionian Sea. The landscape is green — olive groves, cypress forests, orchards — and the air carries the scent of pine and sea salt in equal measure. Combining Parga's coast with Epirus's mountains creates one of the most varied and underrated road trips in Greece.

## Boutique & Hillside Hotels

### Acrothea Hotel

The most polished hotel in Parga — set on the hillside beneath the Venetian castle with an infinity pool that overlooks the harbor, the Ionian Sea, and the small island of Panagia (with its illuminated church) that sits in the bay below. The view from the pool terrace is Parga's best, and it's available every day from a sun lounger.

Rooms are contemporary and well-designed, with private balconies that frame the panorama. The breakfast uses local Epirus products — honey from the mountains, olive oil from the groves, cheese from the shepherds. The service is warm and personal. And the setting — elevated above the harbor bustle, with the castle ruins a short walk above — captures the best of Parga's topography.

**Price range:** €130–300/night
**Best for:** Couples, honeymooners, anyone wanting Parga's best view from a pool terrace
**Good to know:** The hillside location means steps — the harbor is a 5-minute walk downhill (and a more athletic climb uphill). The infinity pool is the hotel's centerpiece. Book the sea-view rooms — the difference defines the stay. Parking is available but limited.

[Check prices for Acrothea Hotel on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/acrothea.html)

### Villa Rossa

A restored stone mansion with a garden courtyard, intimate rooms decorated with antiques and local art, and an atmosphere that feels like staying in a well-traveled friend's Epirus home rather than a commercial hotel. The building has genuine character — wooden ceilings, stone walls, wrought-iron details — and the garden provides a quiet retreat from the harbor's summer bustle.

**Price range:** €100–220/night
**Best for:** Romantic couples, architecture lovers, anyone wanting character over standardized comfort
**Good to know:** Small property — only a handful of rooms. No pool. The garden is the main communal space. The harbor is a short walk. The charm is in the details — period furniture, original stonework, personal hosting.

[Check prices for Villa Rossa on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/villa-rossa-parga.html)

## Town Center Hotels

### Hotel Galini

A well-run family hotel in the heart of Parga — close to the harbor, the lanes, the restaurants, and the town beach — with clean rooms, a pleasant breakfast terrace, and the kind of family hospitality where the owners genuinely care whether you're enjoying yourself. Rooms are well-maintained and functional, some with sea views or balconies overlooking the town.

Hotel Galini occupies the sweet spot that makes Parga work: a quality base at an honest price, positioned so that everything the town offers is walking distance. The harbor tavernas are two minutes away. The castle walk starts nearby. Krioneri Beach is steps from the door.

**Price range:** €70–160/night
**Best for:** Practical travelers, families, couples wanting a central base without overpaying
**Good to know:** Harbor-facing rooms have the best views and more evening noise — choose based on priorities. The town gets lively on summer evenings. Parking in Parga center is tight; the hotel can advise.

[Check prices for Hotel Galini on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/galini-parga.html)

### Pension Acropol

A simple, family-run pension on the castle-hill lanes with clean rooms, a rooftop terrace with panoramic views over the harbor and the Ionian Sea, and prices that leave the budget wide open for boat trips, beachside lunches, and the kind of generous spending that Parga's reasonable restaurants encourage.

The walk up to Acropol is through the lanes that make Parga photogenic — bougainvillea, stone steps, cats in doorways. The terrace at sunset, with a glass of local wine and the harbor lights below, is an experience that no amount of hotel amenities can replicate.

**Price range:** €40–90/night
**Best for:** Budget travelers, solo visitors, couples who don't mind a hill climb for a view
**Good to know:** Basic rooms — clean and functional, nothing more. The rooftop terrace is the attraction. The hill climb is real. No air conditioning in some rooms; fans provided. No pool.

[Check prices for Pension Acropol on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/pension-acropol.html)

## Beach Hotels

### Lichnos Beach Hotel & Suites

The finest beachfront property in the Parga area — set directly on Lichnos Beach, a crescent of golden sand and turquoise water about 3 km south of Parga town. The hotel has been recently renovated with contemporary rooms, a pool, a beach restaurant, and the kind of direct sand-to-room access that makes a beach holiday effortless.

Lichnos is arguably the most beautiful beach in the area — sheltered, sandy, with clear warm water and a setting of green hills that frame the bay. The hotel capitalizes on this setting intelligently, keeping the design simple enough to let the beach do the work.

**Price range:** €120–280/night
**Best for:** Beach lovers, couples, families wanting the area's best sand-and-sea combination
**Good to know:** Lichnos is 3 km from Parga town — a car, taxi, or the summer water-taxi makes the connection easy. The beach is the main amenity. The hotel restaurant is decent; Parga's superior dining is a short drive. The bay is sheltered from the prevailing winds.

[Check prices for Lichnos Beach Hotel on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/lichnos-beach.html)

### Valtos Beach Hotel

Directly on Valtos Beach — Parga's largest and most popular beach, a long curve of sand and pebble on the west side of the headland, reached by a scenic path from the town or by boat-taxi across the harbor. The hotel is practical: pool, restaurant, rooms with beach views, and the simple proposition of sand at your feet.

**Price range:** €80–180/night
**Best for:** Families, beach-first travelers, anyone wanting Valtos's sand without a commute
**Good to know:** Valtos Beach has organized sections (sunbeds, water sports) and free sections. The path to Parga town takes about 15 minutes on foot through olive groves; the boat-taxi takes 5 minutes across the harbor. Evening life is in Parga town.

## Practical Tips for Parga Hotels

**Getting there.** Fly into Aktion/Preveza airport (PVK) — about 1 hour drive to Parga. Charter and budget flights from several European cities in summer. Alternatively, fly into [Corfu](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/corfu-travel-guide) and ferry to Igoumenitsa (about 1 hour), then drive to Parga (about 1 hour). Drive from [Athens](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-athens): about 5 hours via [Ioannina](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/ioannina-travel-guide).

**Day trips.** The Acheron River (mythological entrance to the underworld) is 30 minutes south — kayaking through the gorge is excellent. The Nekromanteion of Acheron (ancient Oracle of the Dead) is nearby. [Paxos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/paxos-travel-guide) island is accessible by boat trip from Parga (a popular day excursion). [Ioannina](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/ioannina-travel-guide) and the [Zagori villages](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/zagori-villages-travel-guide) are 1.5–2 hours east for a mountain day trip.

**When to visit.** June and September are ideal. July–August are busy by Parga standards (the town is popular with Greek and Italian tourists) and hot. May and October are mild and uncrowded. See our [Greece weather guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/greece-weather-by-month).

**Parking.** Parga's center is largely pedestrianized and parking is limited in summer. Most hotels have parking or can advise on public lots at the edges of town. Arrive early in the day or use your hotel's parking solution.

**Combining with Epirus.** The ideal route: [Ioannina](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/ioannina-travel-guide) (lake city, Ottoman heritage, food) → [Zagori villages](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/zagori-villages-travel-guide) and [Vikos Gorge](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/vikos-gorge-travel-guide) (mountains, hiking, stone bridges) → Parga (coast, beaches, harbor). This mountain-to-sea route is one of the most rewarding and least-known in Greece. See our [Greece road trip guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/greece-road-trip).
