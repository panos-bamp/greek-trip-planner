# Best Beaches Near Athens: The Complete Guide to the Attica Coast (2026)

**Subtitle:** Athens is a coastal city — a fact that surprises most first-time visitors who arrive expecting only ruins and museums. The Attica coastline stretches 60km south of the city along the Athens Riviera, offering everything from tram-accessible urban beaches to pine-backed swimming coves that feel like Aegean islands. The problem is that most beach guides list the same 5–6 Riviera spots without telling you how to reach them, which are worth the effort for a visitor with limited time, and which ones require a car. This guide organises Athens's beaches into two clear zones — the tram-accessible Riviera zone and the car-required further beaches — plus the Aegina island option for the best sandy beach day trip from the city.

**Excerpt:** The Athens tram from Syntagma Square to Voula takes 50 minutes and costs €1.20. It passes directly alongside the sea for most of the journey and stops at or near six beaches. This is the single most useful Athens beach logistics fact and it consistently goes underexplained. If you're in Athens for 3–4 days and want a beach afternoon, the tram is the answer — no car, no tour, no planning beyond picking a stop.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The Athens tram is the best tool for Riviera beach access.** The tram line runs from Syntagma Square to Voula (Line 5) and Glyfada along the coast — 50 minutes, €1.20, no transfers needed. It deposits you at or near Edem, Agios Kosmas, Alimos, Glyfada, Voula, and Vouliagmeni beaches. For visitors who want a beach afternoon without renting a car or joining a tour, the tram is the entire answer.
2. **Beaches near Athens divide clearly into two zones.** Zone 1 (Riviera — by public transport): Glyfada, Vouliagmeni, Voula, Varkiza, Edem — all accessible by tram and bus, organised beaches with sunbeds, cafes, and entry fees. Zone 2 (further afield — car required): Schinias/Marathon (best beach, pine forest, 1 hour), Cape Sounion/Kape Beach (archaeology + swimming, 1.5 hours), Porto Rafti (calm family bay, 1 hour). Understanding this structure saves wasted planning time.
3. **Schinias is the best beach near Athens and it requires a car.** The 5km sandy beach backed by a Mediterranean pine forest, with excellent water quality and Blue Flag certification, is genuinely comparable to Cyclades beaches. It's 77km north of Athens near Marathon. Without a car it's inaccessible; with a car it's 55 minutes and worth every minute.
4. **Aegina island is the best sandy beach day trip from Athens** for visitors with more than half a day to spare. The fast catamaran from Piraeus reaches Aegina town in 40 minutes (€15–18 return). Agia Marina beach on the east coast — a long, shallow, sandy bay — is 45 minutes from the port by island bus or taxi. The Temple of Aphaia (one of the best-preserved ancient temples in Greece) sits above Agia Marina. Culture + sand in one day trip from Athens is a specific combination no Riviera beach can match.
5. **Lake Vouliagmeni is not a beach.** It appears on every "beaches near Athens" list, and it is worth visiting, but it is a brackish thermal lake fed by underground springs and sea infiltration — not a sea beach. The thermal mineral waters (constant 22–29°C year-round) have documented spa benefits. Entry is €15/person. It's worth including on an Athens Riviera day but should not replace a beach visit for swimmers.

## ARTICLE BODY

Most visitors to Athens don't realise until they look at a map that the city sits on a coastal promontory, with the Saronic Gulf visible from the Acropolis on a clear day. The city's southern suburbs — Glyfada, Voula, Vouliagmeni, Varkiza — are essentially Athenian beach towns, joined to the centre by the coastal tram.

This guide covers the full beach picture: tram-accessible Riviera beaches (Zone 1), car-required options (Zone 2), and the Aegina island ferry day trip — the best option for visitors specifically seeking quality sand.

## The Athens Tram: Your Key to Zone 1 Beaches

Before listing beaches, the critical logistics fact: **the Athens tram runs directly from Syntagma Square to the coast**, and then along the coast to Glyfada and Voula. It costs €1.20 one-way, runs from approximately 5:30am to 1am (later on weekends), and takes 40–55 minutes depending on your destination stop.

**Tram Line 5** (Syntagma → SEF → Voula): The main coastal line, running along Poseidonos Avenue beside the sea from roughly Faliron to Voula. Stops near beaches: SEF (Edem), Agios Kosmas, Alimos Marina, then Glyfada and Voula sections.

**Transferring to bus for Vouliagmeni:** From the Glyfada tram terminus, bus 122 continues to Vouliagmeni (15 minutes, €1.20 extra). The tram itself doesn't reach Vouliagmeni.

The key insight no competitor article makes explicit: **the tram stop and the beach entrance are often directly adjacent.** Getting off at the correct stop and walking to the sea takes 2–5 minutes. There is no navigation challenge; you can see the water from the tram.


## Zone 1: Riviera Beaches (Public Transport)

### Glyfada Beach

**Distance from centre:** 14km south | **Transport:** Tram (40–45 min) | **Type:** Organised urban beach | **Entry:** Free (public sections) or €8–15 for beach clubs

Glyfada is the most accessible and most popular beach suburb. The beach itself is a long stretch with both organised (paid) sections with sunbeds and umbrellas, and free public areas. The surrounding suburb of Glyfada has excellent restaurants, upscale boutiques, and a lively summer evening scene — making it a full half-day out rather than just a beach visit.

**Best for:** First-timers who want a Riviera beach without logistics. The free beach sections are genuinely usable.

**Avoid:** July-August weekend afternoons — the Glyfada tram terminates in the area, and the beach gets genuinely crowded with Athenians.

---

### Vouliagmeni Beach

**Distance from centre:** 20km south | **Transport:** Tram to Glyfada + bus 122 (55–60 min total) | **Type:** Organised beach club | **Entry:** €10 weekdays / €15 weekends (summer)

Vouliagmeni is consistently rated the finest beach on the immediate Riviera — calmer water (slightly more sheltered bay), excellent facilities, Blue Flag certified, and the upscale Vouliagmeni neighbourhood gives it a more polished feel than Glyfada. The entry fee maintains quality.

**Best for:** Visitors who want the "best" Riviera beach experience and don't mind paying for it.

**Nearby:** Lake Vouliagmeni (see below) is 5 minutes from the beach — combine both in a Vouliagmeni day.

---

### Voula Beaches (A and B)

**Distance from centre:** 18km | **Transport:** Tram to Voula terminus (50 min) | **Entry:** ~€8

Two organised beaches (Voula A and B) managed by the municipality, with better facilities than average and consistent water quality. More of a local beach than a tourist one — fewer visitors than Glyfada but the same tram access.

**Best for:** Families; quieter alternative to Glyfada for the same tram journey.

---

### Edem Beach

**Distance from centre:** 8km | **Transport:** Tram (25 min) | **Type:** Free public beach | **Entry:** Free

The closest sea beach to central Athens — accessible by tram in 25 minutes from Syntagma, free entry, small sandy cove. The water is calm and the beach is popular with students. It's basic (limited facilities) and can be crowded at weekends.

**Best for:** A quick swim on a sightseeing day. Not worth a dedicated half-day; use if you have 2 hours spare rather than a full beach day.

---

### Astir Beach (Vouliagmeni)

**Distance from centre:** 22km | **Transport:** Taxi or car (30 min) | **Entry:** €35+ weekends, €25 weekdays

The most exclusive beach club on the Riviera — private peninsula, 300m of prime beach, luxury loungers, multiple restaurants, water sports. The Four Seasons hotel is adjacent. The entry fee is high but the experience is genuinely different from public Riviera beaches.

**Best for:** Splurge beach day for visitors prioritising amenities over budget.

---

### Lake Vouliagmeni (Not a Beach — A Thermal Lake)

**Distance from centre:** 20km | **Transport:** Tram + bus (55 min) | **Entry:** €15

A brackish lake fed by underground thermal springs and sea filtration, with a constant water temperature of 22–29°C year-round. The mineral-rich water is cloudy blue-green and genuinely therapeutic. The cave system visible from the water is extraordinary.

This is not a sea swimming experience — the water is enclosed, the mineral content is distinctive, and there is no wave action. It is worth visiting for what it is (a natural thermal spa), but it does not replace a beach visit for those specifically seeking open Aegean swimming.

**Best for:** Adding to a Vouliagmeni beach day. Not a standalone beach substitute.


## Zone 2: Car-Required Beaches (Beyond the Riviera)

### Schinias Beach — Best Beach Near Athens

**Distance from Athens:** 77km northeast (Marathon coast) | **Drive time:** 55–65 minutes | **Transport:** Car only (no practical public transport) | **Entry:** Free (no organised sections in main area)

Schinias is the finest beach accessible from Athens and consistently underappreciated in international travel guides. A 5km stretch of sand backed by a protected pine forest (Schinias National Park), with clean blue water, a calm bay, and Blue Flag certification. The combination of shade from the pines, quality sand, and clear water makes it feel more like an Ionian island beach than an Attica coast beach.

The beach has both an organised section (sunbeds, cafes) at the main access points and extensive free sections where you park between the trees and walk directly to the shore. Schinias is popular with Athenians — come on a weekday for the best conditions.

**Bonus:** Nearby Marathon (2km) has the famous Marathon Tomb (ancient burial mound from the Battle of Marathon, 490 BC) — combine beach and history in one trip.

**Best for:** The definitive Athens beach day for anyone with a car.

---

### Cape Sounion / Kape Beach — Archaeology + Swimming Combined

**Distance from Athens:** 70km southeast (Attica tip) | **Drive time:** 1 hour (traffic dependent) | **Transport:** KTEL bus from Pedion Areos (2 hours, hourly) or car | **Entry:** Temple €10; beach free

Cape Sounion is better known for the Temple of Poseidon (Lord Byron carved his name in the marble; the sunset views across the Aegean from the clifftop are extraordinary). But immediately below the headland, the small beach at Kape and the surrounding coves offer exceptional swimming — turquoise water, rocky entries, and the surreal experience of swimming with the temple columns visible above on the cliff.

The KTEL bus (X) from Pedion Areos park in central Athens reaches Cape Sounion in 2 hours — it's one of the few "car-required" Zone 2 beaches that's actually accessible by public transport, albeit slowly.

**Best for:** The single best combination of archaeology and beach swimming near Athens. See also: [Best Day Trips from Athens](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/best-day-trips-from-athens) for the Cape Sounion tour detail.

### Legrena / Kape Beach (South Attica)

**Distance from Athens:** 65km south | **Drive time:** 55 minutes | **Transport:** KTEL bus (1.5 hours, less frequent) or car | **Entry:** Free

Further along the Sounion road, the Legrena coves and Kape beach are among the most beautiful on the Attica coast — small white-sand coves, turquoise water, and a genuine sense of escape from the city. These beaches feel more like Ionian island beaches than Riviera beaches. The water clarity is exceptional.

**Best for:** Travellers willing to go slightly further for a dramatically better beach experience.


## The Aegina Option: Best Sandy Beach Day Trip from Athens

For visitors with a full day and beach quality as the priority, the ferry to Aegina island from Piraeus is the superior option over any Attica mainland beach.

**Ferry logistics:** Fast catamaran from Piraeus (Gate E8/E9) to Aegina Town: 40 minutes, €15–18 return. Regular ferries also run (1 hour, €9). Multiple departures daily from 7am.

**Aegina Town to Agia Marina Beach:** Island bus (45 min, €2.50) or taxi (20 min, €12–15). Agia Marina is the main beach — long, sandy, shallow, and well-served with tavernas and beach facilities.

**Also on Aegina:** The Temple of Aphaia (550 BC, remarkably well-preserved, with views across the Saronic Gulf to Athens) sits above Agia Marina — 15 minutes by taxi or bus from the beach. The combination of a genuine sandy beach and a top-tier archaeological site in one island day trip from Athens is unmatched by any mainland beach alternative.

**Best for:** First-time visitors who want a proper beach day from Athens with island atmosphere, good sand, and some archaeology. The Aegina ferry is the answer.

## Practical Tips

**Timing:** Athens beaches in July-August are genuinely crowded on weekends — Athenians go to the sea every weekend in summer and the Riviera fills from Friday evening. Visit on weekday mornings (before 11am) for the best conditions. Late afternoon (4pm+) often sees crowds thin, especially at free beaches.

**The tram reality check:** The Athens tram is reliable and pleasant for reaching the near Riviera, but it is slow in peak hours (the coastal road can cause delays). The tram is best for a relaxed afternoon — not optimal if you want to arrive at the beach at 10am on a Saturday when it fills.

**Water quality:** The municipality of Athens monitors beach water quality weekly. Blue Flag certified beaches (Voula A, Voula B, Vouliagmeni, parts of Glyfada, Schinias) have tested and verified clean water. Check the current status at swimfo.gr before visiting non-Blue-Flag beaches.

**The "no car" vs "car" decision:** For a half-day beach visit with no car, the tram to Glyfada or Voula is the answer. For a full day and better quality, a car for Schinias is worth it. For the best sandy beach experience from Athens, the Aegina ferry from Piraeus beats everything on the mainland.

## FAQs

**What is the closest beach to Athens?**

Edem beach in Faliro is the closest sea beach to the city centre — accessible by tram from Syntagma in 25 minutes, free entry. For a better beach experience, Glyfada (40 minutes by tram) or Vouliagmeni (55 minutes by tram and bus) offer significantly more facilities and better water.

**Can you swim at Athens beaches?**

Yes — the Athens Riviera beaches are generally clean with monitored water quality. Blue Flag beaches (Vouliagmeni, Voula A and B, parts of Glyfada, Schinias) have verified clean water. The best swimming is from June to October when the sea is 21–26°C.

**How do I get to the beach from Athens by public transport?**

Take the Athens tram from Syntagma Square (Line 5 toward Voula). It runs along the coast to Glyfada and Voula — all beaches along this route are directly accessible. For Vouliagmeni, tram to Glyfada then bus 122 (15 minutes). For Cape Sounion, KTEL bus from Pedion Areos park (2 hours). For Aegina island, metro to Piraeus then ferry.

**What is the best beach near Athens?**

Schinias beach (77km, car required) is the finest overall beach near Athens — long sandy shore backed by pine forest, clean water, and no entry fee. For those without a car, Vouliagmeni beach (tram + bus, 55 minutes) is the best Riviera option. For the best sandy beach experience with island atmosphere, the Aegina ferry from Piraeus (40 minutes) is the superior choice.

**Is Vouliagmeni Beach worth it?**

Yes — Vouliagmeni is the best beach on the immediate Athens Riviera. The entry fee (€10–15) maintains facilities and crowds at manageable levels. The bay is sheltered, water is Blue Flag clean, and the surrounding area has good restaurants. For visitors who want the best Riviera experience without going to Schinias, Vouliagmeni is the answer.
