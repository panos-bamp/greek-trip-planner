# Best Hotels in Serifos, Greece: Our Top Picks for 2026

Serifos is what happens when a Greek island decides it doesn't need to perform. There are no beach bars with DJs. No sunset cocktail brands. No influencer-optimized rooftop pools. What there is: a landscape of startling, mineral beauty — iron-red hills streaked with abandoned mine tunnels, dry-stone walls crossing barren slopes, a coastline of golden-sand beaches that the rest of the Cyclades would kill for — and, sitting improbably on the highest hill, a Chora so white and so vertical that seeing it for the first time from the ferry deck genuinely makes you catch your breath.

The mining history gives Serifos a character unlike any other Cycladic island. Iron ore was extracted here from antiquity until the 1960s, and the ruins of the mines — processing plants, loading docks, tunnels cut into the hillsides — create a kind of industrial archaeology that adds grit to the Cycladic whiteness. The landscape around the mines feels post-apocalyptic in the most beautiful way possible: rusted metal against blue sky, abandoned machinery half-swallowed by scrub, and a silence that carries the weight of departed labor.

The hotel scene has been transformed by a new generation of properties — design-forward, architecturally ambitious, and priced at a fraction of what comparable quality costs on [Mykonos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-mykonos) or [Santorini](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-santorini). Serifos is the island where you get tomorrow's Cycladic hotel at yesterday's prices.

For the full island experience, see our [Serifos travel guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/serifos-travel-guide). This article focuses on the hotels.

## Design & Boutique Hotels

### Coco-Mat Eco Residences

The most ambitious hotel project on Serifos — a collection of stone-built residences above Vagia Bay, designed with the sustainability philosophy of the Coco-Mat brand (known for their natural mattresses and eco-conscious approach). The architecture integrates into the hillside using local stone, the interiors feature natural materials throughout, and the overall aesthetic is a sophisticated marriage of Cycladic tradition and contemporary environmental design.

Each residence has a private terrace, and the views across to the bay and the open Aegean are the kind that make you understand why architects build on hillsides. The beach below is wild and beautiful. The silence is total.

**Price range:** €180–400/night
**Best for:** Design lovers, sustainability-conscious travelers, couples seeking seclusion
**Good to know:** The Vagia Bay location is remote — a car is essential. The remoteness is the point. Livadi port is about 15 minutes by car. The property is self-contained — bring provisions or plan meals at the beach taverna. The Coco-Mat mattresses are genuinely excellent.

[Check prices for Coco-Mat Eco Residences on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/coco-mat-eco-residences-serifos.html)

### Rizes Hotel

A contemporary boutique hotel on the hillside above Livadi port with a pool, well-designed rooms, and views across the harbor to the Chora perched on its hill above. Rizes captures the new Serifos — properties with genuine design ambition that complement the island's raw beauty rather than competing with it. The rooms are Cycladic-white with warm accents, the pool terrace overlooks the port, and the breakfast uses local products.

**Price range:** €130–300/night
**Best for:** Couples, design-conscious travelers, anyone wanting a pool with port-and-Chora views
**Good to know:** The hillside location means a short walk down to Livadi and a steeper walk up. The pool view is the hotel's best feature. A car is useful for the beaches beyond Livadi.

[Check prices for Rizes Hotel on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/rizes-serifos.html)

## Chora Hotels

### Nausicaa Suites

A small, restored property in the heart of Serifos Chora with suites that blend the village's whitewashed architecture with contemporary comfort. The terraces look out over the rooftops to the Aegean — the view that makes the Chora one of the most photographed villages in the western Cyclades. The rooms are intimate and well-designed, with the stone-and-white aesthetic that the building's age demands.

Staying in the Chora means inhabiting the village rather than observing it — the morning light on the lanes, the evening silence broken only by church bells, the star-filled sky that street lighting hasn't yet ruined.

**Price range:** €100–240/night
**Best for:** Couples, photographers, atmosphere seekers, anyone wanting to sleep inside the spectacular Chora
**Good to know:** The Chora is car-free in the center — park at the edge and walk (or take the bus from Livadi). The lanes are steep. Luggage logistics require planning. The Chora has a couple of tavernas and a café; Livadi has more dining options.

[Check prices for Nausicaa Suites on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/nausicaa-suites.html)

## Livadi & Beach Hotels

### Serifos Village

A well-run mid-range hotel on the hillside above Livadi with a pool, comfortable rooms, and the kind of practical, reliable hospitality that makes a week-long stay effortless. The pool area has views across to the Chora. The rooms are well-maintained. The breakfast is adequate. The staff can advise on beaches and boat trips.

**Price range:** €80–180/night
**Best for:** Practical travelers, families, mid-range seekers wanting a pool base near Livadi
**Good to know:** The hillside means a walk to the port. The pool compensates for the distance from the beach. A car is essential for the island's better beaches (Psili Ammos, Ganema, Agios Sostis).

[Check prices for Serifos Village on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/serifos-village.html)

### Coralli Camping & Bungalows (Livadakia)

Not just a campground — Coralli offers simple bungalows directly on Livadakia Beach, a short walk from Livadi port. The bungalows are basic but clean, the beach is golden sand with clear water, and the price makes Serifos accessible to genuinely budget-conscious travelers. The beachfront position — sand at your doorstep — is an amenity that money can't always buy.

**Price range:** €40–100/night (bungalows; camping from €15)
**Best for:** Budget travelers, beach lovers, young couples, anyone wanting sand-at-your-feet simplicity
**Good to know:** Basic bungalows — manage expectations. The beach is the attraction. Livadi's tavernas are a short walk. The atmosphere is relaxed and social.

[Check prices for Coralli on Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/gr/coralli-camping-bungalows.html)

### Maistrali Studios (Livadi)

Simple, clean studios in Livadi port — the most central and affordable base on the island. Kitchenettes, air conditioning, walking distance to the ferry dock and the waterfront tavernas. The price is honest. The rooms are functional. The money you save stays in the tavernas and the beach bars.

**Price range:** €50–120/night
**Best for:** Budget travelers, solo visitors, ferry-connecting stays
**Good to know:** Basic studios. No pool. Livadi port is the island's practical center — restaurants, minimarkets, car rental. The bus to the Chora runs regularly.

## Practical Tips for Serifos Hotels

**Getting there.** Ferry from Piraeus: about 2.5 hours (high-speed) or 4.5 hours (conventional). Serifos is on the western Cyclades route — ferries continue to [Sifnos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/sifnos-travel-guide) (~30 min) and [Milos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-milos) (~1 hour). No airport.

**A car or ATV is essential.** The best beaches — Psili Ammos (the standout, golden sand, turquoise water), Ganema, Agios Sostis, Vagia — are scattered around the island on unpaved roads. A 4x4 or ATV handles the terrain best. Livadi and the Chora are connected by a paved road and regular bus.

**The beaches.** Psili Ammos (southeast coast) is one of the most beautiful beaches in the Cyclades — golden sand, shallow turquoise water, usually uncrowded. Ganema (south coast) is wild and empty. Agios Sostis (northeast) is large and sandy. Livadakia (near the port) is the most accessible.

**When to visit.** June and September are ideal. July–August are the busiest (which on Serifos means pleasantly uncrowded by Cycladic standards) and windiest. May and October are quiet and beautiful. See our [Greece weather guide](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/greece-weather-by-month).

**Combining with other islands.** Serifos fits perfectly into a western Cyclades route: Athens–Serifos–[Sifnos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/sifnos-travel-guide)–[Milos](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/where-to-stay-in-milos)–Athens, or add [Folegandros](https://greektriplanner.me/blog/folegandros-travel-guide) for a wild-Cyclades circuit.
